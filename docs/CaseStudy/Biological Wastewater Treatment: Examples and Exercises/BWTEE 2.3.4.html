Aeration Calculation Method: BioModel​

In the original case, the aeration calculation method was as follows:

  • An 'aeration reaction' line was added in BioModel: kLa⋅(SO2,sat−SO2)k_{L}a·(S_{O2,sat} - S_{O2})kL​a⋅(SO2,sat​−SO2​), where kLak_{L}akL​a was 100h−1100 h^{-1}100h−1.
  • This reaction was then activated in the aeration tank within Project.Activator, thereby achieving the objective of continuously supplying oxygen to the system.

Specifics details: BWTEE 2.3.4 Chemostat.

The Weighted NSE is 0.99994, the slight difference mainly comes from S_O2 (NSE is 0.99922), X_NO2 (NSE is 0.99998) and X_DHO (NSE is 0.99999), and all of the NSE of the others are 1.0.

From the calculation results, it is evident that the Weighted NSE is 0.99994, with minor discrepancies stemming from:

  • S_O2 (with an NSE of 0.99922, 0.28 vs 0.27995)
  • X_NO2 (with an NSE of 0.99998, 0.05415 vs 0.05415)
  • X_DHO (with an NSE of 0.99999, 1.232 vs 1.23248)
  • All other NSE values are 1.0.

This indicates that the simulation results from SIMPO are almost identical to those obtained in AQUASIM.

Aeration Calculation Method: DataSet​

The aforementioned calculation method is more suitable for simple laboratory simulations. In actual engineering, aeration calculations are typically performed within DataSet.

Aeration Calculation Process in SIMPO
SOTR​
  • Under standard conditions (i.e., 20°C and 1 atm), the density of air is approximately 1.204 kg/m³, with an oxygen mass fraction of about 23.15%. This means that 1 m³ of air contains approximately 1.204∗23.15%=0.279kgO21.204 * 23.15\% = 0.279 kgO_21.204∗23.15%=0.279kgO2​.
  • Neglecting the influence of temperature and pressure changes for now, the Standard Oxygen Transfer Rate (SOTR) is:
SOTR(mgO2/d)=0.279(kgO2/m3)∗Qair(m3/d)∗SOTE(%)∗106 SOTR (mgO_2/d) = 0.279 (kgO_2/m³) * Q_{air} (m³/d) * SOTE (\%) * 10^{6}SOTR(mgO2​/d)=0.279(kgO2​/m3)∗Qair​(m3/d)∗SOTE(%)∗106
  • Where:
    • QairQ_{air}Qair​ is the air flow rate, m³/d
    • SOTE is the Standard Oxygen Transfer Efficiency, %
FOTR​
  • Actual reactors typically contain wastewater at non-standard temperatures. Therefore, SOTR must be corrected to obtain the Field Oxygen Transfer Rate (FOTR):
FOTR=dSO2/dt=SOTR∗θ(T−20)∗α∗(β∗SO2,sat−SO2)/SO2,sat,std/V FOTR = dS_{O2}/dt = SOTR * θ^{(T - 20)} * α * (β * S_{O2,sat} - S_{O2}) / S_{O2,sat,std} / VFOTR=dSO2​/dt=SOTR∗θ(T−20)∗α∗(β∗SO2,sat​−SO2​)/SO2,sat,std​/V
  • Where:
    • θ is the temperature correction factor
    • T is the temperature, ℃
    • SO2,sat,stdS_{O2,sat,std}SO2,sat,std​ is the saturated dissolved oxygen concentration in clean water under standard conditions,approximately 9.08~9.17 mg/L, often taken as 9.08 mg/L in engineering practice.
    • α is the Oxygen Transfer Ratio: kLa(Wastewater)/kLa(CleanWater)k_{L}a(Wastewater)/k_{L}a(Clean Water)kL​a(Wastewater)/kL​a(CleanWater)
    • β is the Oxygen Solubility Ratio: SO2,sat(Wastewater)/SO2,sat(CleanWater)S_{O2,sat}(Wastewater)/S_{O2,sat}(Clean Water)SO2,sat​(Wastewater)/SO2,sat​(CleanWater)
    • V is the reactor volume, m³
FOTE​
  • Blower manufacturers provide SOTE-flow curves under standard conditions, which also need to be corrected to the Field Oxygen Transfer Efficiency (FOTE). In SIMPO, the following formula can be fitted based on the SOTE-flow standard condition curve to solve for a, b, and c (this formula ensures a non-linear relationship between air inflow and SOTE, with a decaying-increasing characteristic).
FOTE=a∗(Qd)−b∗Hc=a∗(Qair/d/24)−b∗Hc FOTE = a * (Q_d)^{-b} * H^c = a * (Q_{air} / d / 24)^{-b} * H^cFOTE=a∗(Qd​)−b∗Hc=a∗(Qair​/d/24)−b∗Hc
  • Where:
    • QdQ_dQd​ is the air flow rate per diffuser, m³/h/diffuser
    • d is the number of diffusers
    • H is the diffuser installation depth, m
Solving the Equation​
  • Neglecting the influence of temperature and pressure changes for now, combining the above three equations yields: dSO2/dt=279∗Qair∗a∗(Qair/d/24)−b∗Hc∗α∗(β∗SO2,sat−SO2)/SO2,sat,std/V dS_{O2}/dt = 279 * Q_{air} * a * (Q_{air} / d / 24)^{-b} * H^c * α * (β * S_{O2,sat} - S_{O2}) / S_{O2,sat,std} / VdSO2​/dt=279∗Qair​∗a∗(Qair​/d/24)−b∗Hc∗α∗(β∗SO2,sat​−SO2​)/SO2,sat,std​/V
  • Note that at this point, the unit of dSO2/dtdS_{O2}/dtdSO2​/dt is mgO2/L/dmgO_2/L/dmgO2​/L/d.

In this case, to maintain a kLak_{L}akL​a of 100h−1100 h^{-1}100h−1, we selected the following set of parameters (with an FOTE of 14.1%):

  • Qair: 3215 m³/d
  • d (Tank parameter Diffuser Number): 1
  • H (Tank parameter Diffuser Depth): 0.5 m
  • α (Tank parameter Alpha Factor): 0.55
  • β (Tank parameter Beta Factor): 1
  • a (Pump parameter SOTEa): 0.3259
  • b (Pump parameter SOTEb): 0.1342
  • c (Pump parameter SOTEc): 0.2567
  • V: 0.1 m³
  • SO2,sat,stdS_{O2,sat,std}SO2,sat,std​: 9.08 mg/L

Then:

kLa=279∗3215∗0.3259∗(3215/1/24)−0.1342∗0.50.2567∗0.55/9.08/0.1=76812.63d−1 k_{L}a = 279 * 3215 * 0.3259 * (3215 / 1 / 24) ^ {-0.1342} * 0.5 ^ {0.2567} * 0.55 / 9.08 / 0.1 = 76812.63 d^{-1}kL​a=279∗3215∗0.3259∗(3215/1/24)−0.1342∗0.50.2567∗0.55/9.08/0.1=76812.63d−1

Note that the oxygen unit in the case is mmol/L, thus:

kLa=76812.63/32/24≈100h−1 k_{L}a = 76812.63 / 32 / 24 ≈ 100 h^{-1}kL​a=76812.63/32/24≈100h−1

Specifics details: BWTEE 2.3.4 Chemostat.

From the calculation results, it is evident that the Weighted NSE is 0.99232, with minor discrepancies stemming from:

  • S_O2 (with an NSE of 0.90021, 0.28 vs 0.27877)
  • X_NO2 (with an NSE of 0.99999, 0.05415 vs 0.05416)
  • X_DHO (with an NSE of 0.99999, 1.232 vs 1.23246)
  • All other NSE values are 1.0.

This indicates that the simulation results obtained using this method are also almost identical to those from AQUASIM.

Summary​

In practical use, different methods for aeration calculation can be selected based on the actual situation:

1. For simple laboratory simulations:

  • Use default parameters for DataSet.Tank (i.e., d=0, H=0, α=1, β=1).
  • Add an 'aeration reaction' line in BioModel: kLa⋅(SO2,sat−SO2)k_{L}a·(S_{O2,sat} - S_{O2})kL​a⋅(SO2,sat​−SO2​).
  • Activate this reaction in the aeration tank within Project.Activator.

2. For more precise simulations in engineering projects:

  • Directly fit the parameters a, b, and c in SIMPO based on the manufacturer's SOTE-flow standard condition curve.
  • Connect DataSet.Pump to DataSet.Tank and set the air inflow rate Qair.
  • Set the DataSet.Tank parameters (d, H, α, β) according to actual conditions.
