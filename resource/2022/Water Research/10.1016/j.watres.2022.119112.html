A quantified nitrogen metabolic network by reaction kinetics and mathematical model in a single-stage microaerobic system treating low COD/TN wastewater

DOI: 10.1016/j.watres.2022.119112

Authors: Zhenju Sun, Jianzheng Li, Yiyang Fan, Jia Meng

  • Abstract
  • Objective
  • Solution
  • Experiment
  • Exploration
  • Summary
  • Related

A single-stage intermittent aeration microaerobic reactor (IAMR) has been developed for the cost-effective nitrogen removal from piggery wastewater with a low ratio of chemical oxygen demand (COD) to total nitrogen (TN). In this study, a quantified nitrogen metabolic network was constructed based on the metagenomics, reaction kinetics and mathematical model to provide a revealing insight into the nitrogen removal mechanism in the IAMR. Metagenomics revealed that a complex nitrogen metabolic network, including aerobic ammonia and nitrite oxidation, anammox, denitrification via nitrate and nitrite, and nitrate respiration, existed in the IAMR. A novel method for solving kinetic parameters with high stability was developed based on a genetic algorithm. Use this method to calculate the kinetics of various reactions involved in nitrogen metabolism. Kinetics revealed that simultaneous partial nitritation-anammox (PN/A) and partial denitrification-anammox (PDN/A) were the dominant approaches to nitrogen removal in the IAMR. Finally, a kinetics-based model was proposed for quantitatively describing the nitrogen metabolic network under the limitation of COD. 58% ~ 67% of nitrogen was removed via the anammox-based processes (PN/A and PDN/A), but only 7% ~ 12% and 1% ~ 2% of nitrogen were removed via heterotrophic denitrification of nitrite and nitrate, respectively. The half-inhibition constant of dissolved oxygen (DO) on anammox was simulated as 0.37 ~ 0.60 mg L‚àí 1, filling the gap in quantifying DO inhibition on anammox. High-frequency intermittent aeration was identified as the crucial measure to suppress nitrite-oxidizing bacteria, although it has a high affinity for DO and NO2‚àí -N. In continuous aeration mode, the simulated NO3‚àí -N in the IAMR would rise by 39.6%. The research provides a novel insight into the nitrogen removal mechanism in single-stage microaerobic systems and provides a reliable approach to practicing PN/A and PDN/A for cost-effective nitrogen removal.

Q: What problem does this paper attempt to solve?

Gemini 3.0 Pro:

The paper attempts to solve the problem of understanding and quantifying the complex nitrogen removal mechanisms in a single-stage intermittent aeration microaerobic reactor (IAMR) treating piggery wastewater with a low COD/TN ratio. Specifically, it addresses:

  • The lack of quantified nitrogen metabolic networks in microaerobic processes.
  • The difficulty in determining stable kinetic parameters for various nitrogen transformation reactions (especially under specific environmental conditions like low COD).
  • The gap in quantifying the dissolved oxygen (DO) inhibition constant for anammox bacteria.
  • The need for a reliable mathematical model to describe the system's performance and the contribution of different pathways (PN/A vs. PDN/A).

Q: How the paper solves this problem?

Gemini 3.0 Pro:

The paper employs a combined approach of metagenomics, kinetic experiments, and mathematical modeling:

  • Metagenomics: Used to identify the microbial community and metabolic pathways (genes) present in the IAMR sludge.
  • Kinetic Assays: Conducted batch experiments to measure Oxygen Uptake Rates (OUR) and substrate utilization rates for specific processes (Ammonia oxidation, Nitrite oxidation, Anammox, Heterotrophic denitrification, etc.).
  • Algorithm Development: Developed a novel algorithm based on Genetic Algorithm (GA) and the finite element method to solve Monod kinetic parameters (Œºmax\mu_{max}Œºmax‚Äã and KSK_SKS‚Äã) with higher stability than traditional linear fitting.
  • Mathematical Modeling: Constructed a kinetics-based mathematical model incorporating the derived parameters and correction factors for COD limitation and DO inhibition to simulate the nitrogen flux and quantify the contribution of each pathway.

Q: What are the points that can be explored further?

Gemini 3.0 Pro:

  • Long-term Stability of PDN/A: While the study highlights PDN/A's role, the long-term stability of the nitrate respiration (DNRA) pathway coupling with Anammox under fluctuating influent conditions could be explored.
  • Microbial Community Dynamics: Further investigation into how the microbial community structure shifts dynamically in response to changes in aeration frequency or COD/TN ratios over extended periods.
  • Application to Different Wastewaters: Testing the developed GA-based parameter estimation method and the metabolic model on other types of high-strength or low-carbon wastewaters.
  • Optimization of Intermittent Aeration: Fine-tuning the aeration/non-aeration cycles based on the model predictions to further minimize nitrate production while maintaining high ammonia oxidation rates.
