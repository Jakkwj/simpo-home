Evaluation of Nitrous Oxide Emission from Sulfide- and Sulfur-Based Autotrophic Denitrification Processes

DOI: 10.1021/acs.est.6b02202

Authors: Yiwen Liu, Lai Peng, Huu Hao Ngo, Wenshan Guo, Dongbo Wang, Yuting Pan, Jing Sun, and Bing-Jie Ni

Recent studies have shown that sulfide- and sulfur-based autotrophic denitrification (AD) processes play an important role in contributing to nitrous oxide (N2O) production and emissions. However, N2O production is not recognized in the current AD models, limiting their ability to predict N2O accumulation during AD. In this work, a mathematical model is developed to describe N2O dynamics during sulfide- and sulfur-based AD processes for the first time. The model is successfully calibrated and validated using N2O data from two independent experimental systems with sulfide or sulfur as electron donors for AD. The model satisfactorily describes nitrogen reductions, sulfide/sulfur oxidation, and N2O accumulation in both systems. Modeling results revealed substantial N2O accumulation due to the relatively low N2O reduction rate during both sulfide- and sulfur-based AD processes. Application of the model to simulate long-term operations of activated sludge systems performing sulfide- and sulfur-based AD processes indicates longer sludge retention time reduced N2O emission. For sulfide-based AD process, higher initial S/N ratio also decreased N2O emission but with a higher operational cost. This model can be a useful tool to support process operation optimization for N2O mitigation during AD with sulfide or sulfur as electron donor.

Q: What are the points that can be explored further?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

  • Integration with heterotrophic denitrification N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO models: The authors note that for high-concentration nitrate removal, a carbon source is often co-supplied, leading to simultaneous heterotrophic and autotrophic denitrification. The current model does not account for competition between heterotrophic and autotrophic denitrifiers for nitrogen compounds. Future work could integrate this model with ASM-based heterotrophic denitrifying N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO models (e.g., Ni et al., 2011; Liu et al., 2015) to predict overall N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO dynamics in combined systems.

  • Effect of pH on N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emissions: The paper briefly mentions that lower pH could result in more free nitrous acid (FNA), potentially inhibiting the AD process and leading to higher nitrite accumulation and more N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emissions. A systematic modeling study of pH effects on N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production in AD processes is warranted.

  • Incorporation of NO dynamics: Nitric oxide (NO) was excluded from the model because NO reduction-related parameters are beyond current measurement capabilities. Future advances in measurement technology could allow NO to be incorporated as an intermediate, improving model accuracy.

  • Sulfur particle characteristics: The paper discusses how sulfur surface area, morphology, particle size, and source (in situ generated vs. externally added) affect sulfur mass transfer and AD reaction rates. A more detailed sub-model for sulfur dissolution/mass transfer could improve model predictions, especially for sulfur-based AD systems.

  • Microbial community dynamics: The paper acknowledges that differences in microbial community structure (e.g., Thiobacillus, Sulfurimonas for sulfur-based AD; Thiomicrospira, Arcobacter, Thiobacillus for sulfide-based AD) likely determine differences in kinetic parameters. Future models could incorporate microbial community shifts under varying operational conditions.

  • Sulfate production and its management: Both AD processes produce large amounts of SO42‚àíSO_4^{2-}SO42‚àí‚Äã. The U.S. EPA limit is 250 mg/L. Future studies could model the trade-off between nitrate removal efficiency, N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emission, and sulfate production to find optimal operating windows.

  • Long-term SRT effects on sludge quality: The paper notes that extended SRT may reduce active biomass fraction and increase soluble microbial products (SMP) in the effluent. A model coupling N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO dynamics with SMP production and sludge activity would be valuable.

  • Real-world field-scale validation: The model was calibrated and validated using laboratory-scale batch experiments. Validation with full-scale or pilot-scale continuous-flow systems under real groundwater conditions would strengthen its applicability.

  • Thiosulfate and sulfite as intermediates: The current model excludes thiosulfate (S2O32‚àíS_2O_3^{2-}S2‚ÄãO32‚àí‚Äã) and sulfite (SO32‚àíSO_3^{2-}SO32‚àí‚Äã) as they are considered chemical oxidation intermediates. Future models could include these species for a more complete description of the sulfur cycle, especially under conditions where chemical oxidation is significant.

Gemini 3.1 Pro:

  • The effect of simultaneous heterotrophic and autotrophic denitrification processes on N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emissions, especially when external carbon is added to control sulfate formation.
  • The impact of pH variations on free nitrous acid formation and its subsequent inhibition on the AD process and N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation.
  • Integration of the developed model with standard ASM-based heterotrophic denitrifying N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO models for comprehensive wastewater treatment plant simulations.

Gemini 3.0 Pro:

  • Microbial Community Linkages: The paper suggests that differences in kinetic parameters between sulfide- and sulfur-based systems are likely due to different dominant microbial communities (e.g., Thiobacillus, Sulfurimonas, Arcobacter). Further research could explicitly link community structure to model kinetics.
  • Sulfur Characteristics: The effect of sulfur particle size, morphology, and source (biogenic vs. chemical) on mass transfer and reaction rates in sulfur-based AD needs further exploration.
  • Mixotrophic Conditions: Modeling systems where both organic carbon and reduced sulfur compounds are present (simultaneous heterotrophic and autotrophic denitrification).
  • pH and Inhibition: The influence of pH, particularly regarding free nitrous acid (FNA) inhibition on N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO reduction, could be integrated.
  • Integration with ASM: Combining this model with standard Activated Sludge Models (ASM) for comprehensive plant-wide modeling.

Q: Summarize the main points of the paper.

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

  • First mathematical model for N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO dynamics in sulfide- and sulfur-based AD: This paper presents the first model capable of predicting N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production and accumulation during autotrophic denitrification (AD) processes using sulfide (S2‚àíS^{2-}S2‚àí) or elemental sulfur (S0S^0S0) as electron donors.

  • Model structure: The model incorporates three-step denitrification (NO3‚àí‚ÜíNO2‚àí‚ÜíN2O‚ÜíN2NO_3^- \rightarrow NO_2^- \rightarrow N_2O \rightarrow N_2NO3‚àí‚Äã‚ÜíNO2‚àí‚Äã‚ÜíN2‚ÄãO‚ÜíN2‚Äã) and two-step sulfide oxidation (S2‚àí‚ÜíS0‚ÜíSO42‚àíS^{2-} \rightarrow S^0 \rightarrow SO_4^{2-}S2‚àí‚ÜíS0‚ÜíSO42‚àí‚Äã), resulting in 6 biological reaction processes plus biomass decay, described by double Monod-type kinetics within an ASM framework.

  • Key finding ‚Äì low N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO reduction rate causes accumulation: Modeling results revealed that substantial N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation occurs in both sulfide- and sulfur-based AD processes due to the relatively low N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO reduction rate (process 3 and 6). The estimated ŒºSOB,3S2\mu_{SOB,3}^{S2}ŒºSOB,3S2‚Äã (0.076 h‚àí1^{-1}‚àí1) and ŒºSOB,3S0\mu_{SOB,3}^{S0}ŒºSOB,3S0‚Äã (0.010‚Äì0.017 h‚àí1^{-1}‚àí1) are significantly lower than the corresponding rates for NO3‚àíNO_3^-NO3‚àí‚Äã and NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã reduction, and also lower than the anoxic growth rate on N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO by heterotrophic denitrifiers (~0.134 h‚àí1^{-1}‚àí1).

  • Successful calibration and validation: The model was calibrated and validated using independent experimental datasets from two different AD cultures (sulfide-based granular sludge and sulfur-based fluidized bed MBR culture) under various conditions (different granule sizes, S/N ratios, and initial nitrate concentrations), demonstrating its robustness and wide applicability.

  • Effect of SRT on N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emission: Model simulations of continuous-flow systems showed that increasing SRT from 25 to 250 days significantly reduced N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emission factors: from ~2% to ~0.2% for sulfide-based AD, and from ~18% to ~2% for sulfur-based AD, while maintaining >95% nitrate removal.

  • Effect of S/N ratio on N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emission (sulfide-based AD): Higher initial S/N ratios (e.g., from 2.5 to 6 g S/(g N)) substantially decreased N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emission (from ~1.9% to ~0.1%) in sulfide-based AD, due to the faster autotrophic N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO reduction rate with sulfide.
