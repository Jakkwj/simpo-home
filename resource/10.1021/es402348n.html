Modeling Electron Competition among Nitrogen Oxides Reduction and N2O Accumulation in Denitrification

DOI: 10.1021/es402348n

Authors: Yuting Pan, Bing-Jie Ni, and Zhiguo Yuan

Competition for electrons among different steps of denitrification has previously been shown to occur, and to play an important role in the accumulation and emission of N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO in wastewater treatment. However, this electron competition is not recognized in the current denitrification models, limiting their ability to predict N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation during denitrification. In this work, a new denitrification model is developed for wastewater treatment processes. It describes electron competition among the four steps of denitrification, through modeling the carbon oxidation and nitrogen reduction processes separately, in contrast to the existing models that directly couple these two types of processes. Electron carriers are introduced to link carbon oxidation, which donates electrons to carriers, and nitrogen oxides reduction, which receives electrons from these carriers. The relative ability of each denitrification step to compete for electrons is modeled through the use of different affinity constants with reduced carriers. Model calibration and validation results demonstrate that the developed model is able to reasonably describe the nitrate, nitrite, and N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO reduction rates of a methanol-utilizing denitrifying culture under various carbon and nitrogen oxides supplying conditions. The model proposed, while subject to further validation, is expected to enhance our ability to predict N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation in denitrification.

Q: What problem does this paper attempt to solve?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

  • Inability of existing denitrification models to predict N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation: Existing multi-step denitrification models (e.g., ASM1, ASM2, ASM3, ASMN) directly couple carbon oxidation and nitrogen oxide reduction into a single oxidation-reduction reaction, ignoring the fact that these two processes are carried out by different enzymes with distinct kinetics.
  • Electron competition among denitrification steps is not modeled: In conventional models, each denitrification step is treated independently, with no mechanism to reflect competition for electrons among the four reduction steps (NO3‚àí‚ÜíNO2‚àí‚ÜíNO‚ÜíN2O‚ÜíN2NO_3^- \rightarrow NO_2^- \rightarrow NO \rightarrow N_2O \rightarrow N_2NO3‚àí‚Äã‚ÜíNO2‚àí‚Äã‚ÜíNO‚ÜíN2‚ÄãO‚ÜíN2‚Äã). This means the models cannot capture the scenario where limited electron supply (from carbon oxidation) leads to unequal electron distribution among the reductases.
  • N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation under carbon-limiting conditions is not predictable: When the COD/N ratio is low, or when slowly biodegradable intracellular carbon (e.g., PHB) is used as electron donor, the electron supply rate may be insufficient to meet the demand of all four reduction steps simultaneously, leading to N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation. Existing models assume electron supply always meets total electron demand, which is fundamentally flawed under such conditions.

In summary, the paper aims to develop a new mechanistic denitrification model that explicitly incorporates electron competition among the four denitrification steps, enabling more accurate prediction of N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation during heterotrophic denitrification in wastewater treatment.

Q: How the paper solves this problem?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

The paper develops a new denitrification model by decoupling carbon oxidation from nitrogen oxide reduction and linking them through an electron carrier pool. The key innovations are:

1. Introduction of Electron Carriers (MredM_{red}Mred‚Äã and MoxM_{ox}Mox‚Äã)‚Äã

  • Two new state variables, SMredS_{M_{red}}SMred‚Äã‚Äã (reduced electron carrier) and SMoxS_{M_{ox}}SMox‚Äã‚Äã (oxidized electron carrier), are introduced to represent the intracellular electron carrier pool (analogous to the NADH/NAD+NAD^+NAD+ pool).
  • The total carrier concentration is conserved: SMred+SMox=CtotS_{M_{red}} + S_{M_{ox}} = C_{tot}SMred‚Äã‚Äã+SMox‚Äã‚Äã=Ctot‚Äã

2. Decoupled Process Structure (6 Processes + 1 Constraint)‚Äã

  • Carbon oxidation (R1 & R2): Methanol catabolism and anabolism are modeled separately. Both processes consume MoxM_{ox}Mox‚Äã and produce MredM_{red}Mred‚Äã, representing electron donation from carbon oxidation to the carrier pool.
  • Nitrogen reduction (R3‚ÄìR6): Four sequential steps each consume MredM_{red}Mred‚Äã and regenerate MoxM_{ox}Mox‚Äã:
    • R3: NO3‚àíNO_3^-NO3‚àí‚Äã ‚Üí NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã (nitrate reductase, Nar)
    • R4: NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã ‚Üí NO (nitrite reductase, Nir)
    • R5: NO ‚Üí N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO (NO reductase, Nor)
    • R6: N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO ‚Üí N2N_2N2‚Äã (N2O reductase, Nos)

3. Modeling Electron Competition via Differential Affinity Constants‚Äã

  • Each nitrogen oxide reductase has its own affinity constant for MredM_{red}Mred‚Äã: KMred,1K_{M_{red},1}KMred‚Äã,1‚Äã, KMred,2K_{M_{red},2}KMred‚Äã,2‚Äã, KMred,3K_{M_{red},3}KMred‚Äã,3‚Äã, KMred,4K_{M_{red},4}KMred‚Äã,4‚Äã.
  • A lower KMredK_{M_{red}}KMred‚Äã‚Äã value indicates a higher affinity for electrons, meaning that enzyme can outcompete others when SMredS_{M_{red}}SMred‚Äã‚Äã is low.
  • The calibrated values show KMred,2‚âà3.93√ó10‚àí4K_{M_{red},2} \approx 3.93 \times 10^{-4}KMred‚Äã,2‚Äã‚âà3.93√ó10‚àí4 (Nir) is ~10 times smaller than KMred,1K_{M_{red},1}KMred‚Äã,1‚Äã (Nar) and KMred,4K_{M_{red},4}KMred‚Äã,4‚Äã (Nos), indicating nitrite reductase has the highest electron affinity and thus preferentially captures electrons under electron-limited conditions, explaining observed N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation.

4. Kinetic Rate Expressions (Monod-type)‚Äã

  • Each process uses Monod kinetics with respect to both the substrate (nitrogen oxide or carbon source) and the electron carrier (MredM_{red}Mred‚Äã or MoxM_{ox}Mox‚Äã).
  • This structure allows the model to naturally simulate electron competition: when SMredS_{M_{red}}SMred‚Äã‚Äã is low, all four reduction steps are limited, but the one with the lowest KMredK_{M_{red}}KMred‚Äã‚Äã (Nir) is least affected, leading to preferential nitrite reduction and N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation.

  • 17 parameters in total: 6 from literature, 5 assumed (with sensitivity analysis), and 6 calibrated using experimental data from 14 batch/fed-batch tests.
  • Parameter estimation performed using AQUASIM software by minimizing sum of squared deviations between measured and predicted NO3‚àíNO_3^-NO3‚àí‚Äã, NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã, and N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO profiles.
  • Model validated against 21 additional fed-batch tests under various methanol loading rates.

Q: What are the points that can be explored further?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

  • Validation with other carbon sources: The model was calibrated and validated only with methanol as the sole electron donor. Further validation using other carbon sources (e.g., acetate, ethanol, glucose, PHB) and real wastewater sludge from full-scale treatment plants is needed to generalize the model.

  • Integration with nitrifier-N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO models: The paper suggests that integrating this denitrification model with existing nitrifier-N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO models (e.g., those proposed by Ni et al.) would create a comprehensive tool to estimate overall N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emission factors and explore the effect of operational conditions on N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO dynamics in full-scale wastewater treatment systems.

  • Experimental determination of rN2O,maxr_{N_2O,max}rN2‚ÄãO,max‚Äã: Sensitivity analysis shows that KMred,4K_{M_{red},4}KMred‚Äã,4‚Äã is highly sensitive to the assumed value of rN2O,maxr_{N_2O,max}rN2‚ÄãO,max‚Äã. Developing experimental methods to directly measure the true maximum N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO reductase activity (Nos) would significantly improve model accuracy and reduce parameter uncertainty.

  • Modeling the effect of environmental factors (pH, DO, sulfide) on electron competition: The paper briefly discusses that pH, dissolved oxygen, and sulfide may interact with electron competition to cause N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation. Incorporating these inhibition/stimulation effects into the electron competition framework would improve the model's applicability under varying environmental conditions.

  • Application to full-scale wastewater treatment plants: The model has only been tested at lab scale. Applying and validating it with data from full-scale biological nitrogen removal plants would be a critical step toward practical engineering use.

  • Mechanistic representation of individual electron carriers: The model uses lumped parameters MredM_{red}Mred‚Äã and MoxM_{ox}Mox‚Äã to represent all electron carriers. A more detailed mechanistic model distinguishing individual carriers (e.g., PQQ, NADH/NAD+NAD^+NAD+, various cytochromes) could provide deeper biochemical insight, though at the cost of increased model complexity.

  • Modeling electron competition under transient conditions: The current model was validated under relatively controlled batch/fed-batch conditions. Its performance under dynamic, transient conditions (e.g., fluctuating COD/N ratios, intermittent aeration) in real systems warrants further investigation.

  • Identifiability of CtotC_{tot}Ctot‚Äã: Sensitivity analysis shows that KMred,1K_{M_{red},1}KMred‚Äã,1‚Äã, KMred,2K_{M_{red},2}KMred‚Äã,2‚Äã, and KMred,4K_{M_{red},4}KMred‚Äã,4‚Äã scale proportionally with CtotC_{tot}Ctot‚Äã. Developing methods to experimentally measure intracellular electron carrier concentrations in mixed denitrifying cultures would reduce the need for assumptions and improve parameter identifiability.

Q: Summarize the main points of the paper.

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

This paper presents a novel mathematical model for heterotrophic denitrification that explicitly incorporates electron competition among the four sequential denitrification steps to predict N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation in wastewater treatment. The key contributions and findings are:

1. Identification of the Limitation of Existing Models‚Äã

  • Existing multi-step denitrification models (including ASMN) directly couple carbon oxidation and nitrogen oxide reduction, ignoring the possibility that electron supply can be a rate-limiting step.
  • These models cannot predict N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation under carbon-limiting conditions because they do not model electron competition.

2. New Model Structure with Electron Carriers‚Äã

  • The model decouples carbon oxidation (R1, R2) from nitrogen oxide reduction (R3‚ÄìR6) by introducing lumped electron carrier variables MredM_{red}Mred‚Äã and MoxM_{ox}Mox‚Äã.
  • Carbon oxidation produces MredM_{red}Mred‚Äã; each of the four nitrogen oxide reduction steps consumes MredM_{red}Mred‚Äã and regenerates MoxM_{ox}Mox‚Äã.
  • The total carrier pool is conserved: SMred+SMox=CtotS_{M_{red}} + S_{M_{ox}} = C_{tot}SMred‚Äã‚Äã+SMox‚Äã‚Äã=Ctot‚Äã.

3. Electron Competition Modeled via Differential Affinity Constants‚Äã

  • Four affinity constants (KMred,1K_{M_{red},1}KMred‚Äã,1‚Äã, KMred,2K_{M_{red},2}KMred‚Äã,2‚Äã, KMred,3K_{M_{red},3}KMred‚Äã,3‚Äã, KMred,4K_{M_{red},4}KMred‚Äã,4‚Äã) for MredM_{red}Mred‚Äã are introduced for the four reductases (Nar, Nir, Nor, Nos).

4. Model Calibration and Validation‚Äã

  • The model contains 17 parameters; 6 calibrated, 6 from literature, 5 assumed with sensitivity analysis.

  • Model predictions match experimental NO3‚àíNO_3^-NO3‚àí‚Äã, NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã, and N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO profiles well across all 35 tests and 5 methanol supply schemes.
