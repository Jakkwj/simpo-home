Modeling Electron Competition among Nitrogen Oxides Reduction and N2O Accumulation in Denitrification

DOI: 10.1021/es402348n

Authors: Yuting Pan, Bing-Jie Ni, and Zhiguo Yuan

Competition for electrons among different steps of denitrification has previously been shown to occur, and to play an important role in the accumulation and emission of N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO in wastewater treatment. However, this electron competition is not recognized in the current denitrification models, limiting their ability to predict N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation during denitrification. In this work, a new denitrification model is developed for wastewater treatment processes. It describes electron competition among the four steps of denitrification, through modeling the carbon oxidation and nitrogen reduction processes separately, in contrast to the existing models that directly couple these two types of processes. Electron carriers are introduced to link carbon oxidation, which donates electrons to carriers, and nitrogen oxides reduction, which receives electrons from these carriers. The relative ability of each denitrification step to compete for electrons is modeled through the use of different affinity constants with reduced carriers. Model calibration and validation results demonstrate that the developed model is able to reasonably describe the nitrate, nitrite, and N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO reduction rates of a methanol-utilizing denitrifying culture under various carbon and nitrogen oxides supplying conditions. The model proposed, while subject to further validation, is expected to enhance our ability to predict N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO accumulation in denitrification.

Q: What problem does this paper attempt to solve?

The paper attempts to solve the limitation of existing denitrification models (such as the Activated Sludge Models, ASMs) which fail to accurately predict nitrous oxide (N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO) accumulation, particularly under electron-limiting conditions.

  • Lack of Mechanism: Current models directly couple carbon oxidation and nitrogen reduction, ignoring the competition for electrons among the four reduction steps (NO3‚àí‚ÜíNO2‚àí‚ÜíNO‚ÜíN2O‚ÜíN2NO_3^- \to NO_2^- \to NO \to N_2O \to N_2NO3‚àí‚Äã‚ÜíNO2‚àí‚Äã‚ÜíNO‚ÜíN2‚ÄãO‚ÜíN2‚Äã).

Q: What experiments were done for the paper?

Gemini 3.0 Pro:

The paper utilized extensive experimental data previously reported by the authors (Pan et al., 2013) using a methanol-utilizing denitrifying culture in a lab-scale sequencing batch reactor (SBR). The experiments included:

  • 35 Total Tests: Conducted under anaerobic conditions.
  • Variable Electron Supply: Methanol was supplied either via pulse feeding (non-limiting) or continuous feeding at four different loading rates (6.3, 3.9, 1.1, 0.7 mmol/(gVSS √ó\times√ó h)) to create varying degrees of electron limitation.

  • Model Evaluation: 14 tests were used for model calibration (parameter estimation), and the remaining 21 tests were used for model validation.

Q: What are the points that can be explored further?

Gemini 3.0 Pro:

The authors identify several areas for future research and model improvement:

  • Different Carbon Sources: The current model was validated with methanol; it needs to be tested with other carbon sources common in wastewater.
  • Full-Scale Application: Validation is needed using mixed culture sludge from full-scale wastewater treatment plants, rather than just enriched lab cultures.
  • Integration: The model should be integrated with nitrifier-N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO models to provide a comprehensive tool for predicting overall N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emissions from wastewater treatment systems.
  • Parameter Refinement: The parameters for NO reduction (rNO,maxr_{NO,max}rNO,max‚Äã and KMred,3K_{Mred,3}KMred,3‚Äã) were estimated based on the absence of NO accumulation; direct measurement of NO kinetics would refine these values.

  • New Model Structure: A denitrification model was developed that separates carbon oxidation and nitrogen reduction, linked by an electron carrier pool (Mred/MoxM_{red}/M_{ox}Mred‚Äã/Mox‚Äã).
  • Electron Competition: The model successfully describes electron competition by using different affinity constants (KMredK_{Mred}KMred‚Äã) for each reduction step.
