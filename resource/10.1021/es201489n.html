Nitrous oxide (N2O) can be formed during biological nitrogen (N) removal processes. In this work, a mathematical model is developed that describes N2O production and consumption during activated sludge nitrification and denitrification. The well-known ASM process models are extended to capture N2O dynamics during both nitrification and denitrification in biological N removal. Six additional processes and three additional reactants, all involved in known biochemical reactions, have been added. The validity and applicability of the model is demonstrated by comparing simulations with experimental data on N2O production from four different mixed culture nitrification and denitrification reactor study reports. Modeling results confirm that hydroxylamine oxidation by ammonium oxidizers (AOB) occurs 10 times slower when NO2- participates as final electron acceptor compared to the oxic pathway. Among the four denitrification steps, the last one (N2O reduction to N2) seems to be inhibited first when O2 is present. Overall, N2O production can account for 0.1-25% of the consumed N in different nitrification and denitrification systems, which can be well simulated by the proposed model. In conclusion, we provide a modeling structure, which adequately captures N2O dynamics in autotrophic nitrification and heterotrophic denitrification driven biological N removal processes and which can form the basis for ongoing refinements.

This paper attempts to solve the lack of a comprehensive and mechanistically sound mathematical model for describing N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production and consumption during biological nitrogen (N) removal processes in activated sludge systems. Specifically:

  • N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO is a potent greenhouse gas (300√ó stronger radiative forcing than CO2CO_2CO2‚Äã) and contributes to stratospheric ozone depletion, making even small emissions environmentally significant.

  • There was no generic model that integrates both nitrification-driven and denitrification-driven N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO dynamics within the well-established ASM (Activated Sludge Model) framework, applicable to mixed-culture systems under a wide range of operational conditions.

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

1. AOB Growth under Anoxic Conditions:

  • The current model does not consider biomass growth of AOB coupled with NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã respiration under limiting DO concentrations. As more information on anoxic AOB growth becomes available, this simplification should be revised.

  • The model ignores the direct chemical/enzymatic production of N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO from NH2OHNH_2OHNH2‚ÄãOH via HAO (i.e., Œ±=0\alpha = 0Œ±=0 in Figure 1A). 3. N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO Production during Aerobic Recovery after Anoxia:

  • The model cannot capture N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production during the transition from anoxic to aerobic conditions, a phenomenon observed by Yu et al. (2010). This transient behavior requires further mechanistic understanding and model refinement.

4. Validation of NO Dynamics:

  • While NO is incorporated as a model component (as a precursor to N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO), NO dynamics were not experimentally validated in this study. Future work should include simultaneous measurements of NO and N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO to validate the NO sub-model.

6. Parameter Universality and Microbial Community Effects:

  • Model parameter values vary across the four case studies, possibly due to differences in microbial community composition under different reactor conditions. Future studies should investigate the relationship between microbial community structure and N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production kinetics.

7. Full-Scale BNR Application:

  • The model has not yet been validated for precise quantitative prediction of N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emissions in full-scale biological nutrient removal (BNR) plants. Continued testing against full-scale data is needed to delineate parameter value ranges and confirm consensus mechanisms.

8. Integration with Other N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO Production Mechanisms:

  • The relative importance of the two AOB pathways (nitrifier denitrification vs. HAO-mediated NH2OHNH_2OHNH2‚ÄãOH oxidation) under different DO conditions needs further investigation, as they are likely differently affected by dissolved oxygen concentrations.

9. Anammox and NOB Contributions:

  • The paper notes that NOB and anaerobic ammonia oxidizers (anammox) are currently not considered significant N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO contributors. However, as anammox-based processes become more prevalent in wastewater treatment, their potential N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production should be investigated and potentially incorporated into the model framework.

  • AOB (nitrifier denitrification): Under low DO, NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã serves as terminal electron acceptor; NH2OHNH_2OHNH2‚ÄãOH is oxidized via HAO, and electrons are used to reduce NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã ‚Üí NO ‚Üí N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO via NIR and NOR. The HAO-mediated direct N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production from NH2OHNH_2OHNH2‚ÄãOH is excluded based on current evidence.
  • Heterotrophic denitrification (four-step): Sequential reduction NO3‚àí‚ÜíNO2‚àí‚ÜíNO‚ÜíN2O‚ÜíN2NO_3^- \rightarrow NO_2^- \rightarrow NO \rightarrow N_2O \rightarrow N_2NO3‚àí‚Äã‚ÜíNO2‚àí‚Äã‚ÜíNO‚ÜíN2‚ÄãO‚ÜíN2‚Äã, each step with individual reaction-specific kinetics and NO inhibition for the last three steps.

3. Key Modeling Findings:

  • The anoxic reduction factor Œ∑AOB\eta_{AOB}Œ∑AOB‚Äã was calibrated in the range of 0.05‚Äì0.09, confirming that AOB activity is ~10 times lower when NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã (vs. O2O_2O2‚Äã) is the final electron acceptor.
  • Among the four denitrification steps, the last step (N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO reduction to N2N_2N2‚Äã) is inhibited first by O2O_2O2‚Äã, consistent with experimental observations.
  • N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production can account for 0.1‚Äì25% of consumed N across different nitrification and denitrification systems.
  • In continuous reactor simulations, nitritation produces higher N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO (0.5‚Äì11.5% of consumed NH4+NH_4^+NH4+‚Äã) than heterotrophic denitrification (2.6‚Äì16.8% of consumed NO3‚àíNO_3^-NO3‚àí‚Äã under low COD/NCOD/NCOD/N).

4. Model Validation against Four Case Studies:

  • The model was successfully calibrated and validated against experimental data from four diverse systems (SBR, continuous-flow; domestic wastewater, synthetic wastewater; batch and continuous operation).
  • High R2R^2R2 values (>0.85 for most variables) demonstrate good agreement between model predictions and experimental observations.
  • Parameter identifiability was rigorously confirmed using 95% confidence intervals and joint confidence ellipsoids.

5. Operational Insights:

  • Low DO concentrations significantly increase N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production during nitrification/nitritation.
  • High NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã accumulation is a key driver of N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production by AOB.
  • Low COD/NCOD/NCOD/N ratios in denitrification lead to significantly higher N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emissions.
  • N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production can be controlled by optimizing DO set points and COD/NCOD/NCOD/N ratios.

6. Significance:

  • This is one of the first comprehensive, mechanistically-based models that integrates both nitrification and denitrification N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO dynamics within the ASM framework, with rigorous parameter estimation and independent validation across multiple experimental systems.
