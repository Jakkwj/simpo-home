A quantified nitrogen metabolic network by reaction kinetics and mathematical model in a single-stage microaerobic system treating low COD/TN wastewater

DOI: 10.1016/j.watres.2022.119112

Authors: Zhenju Sun, Jianzheng Li, Yiyang Fan, Jia Meng

  • Abstract
  • Objective
  • Solution
  • Experiment
  • Exploration
  • Summary
  • Related

A single-stage intermittent aeration microaerobic reactor (IAMR) has been developed for the cost-effective nitrogen removal from piggery wastewater with a low ratio of chemical oxygen demand (COD) to total nitrogen (TN). In this study, a quantified nitrogen metabolic network was constructed based on the metagenomics, reaction kinetics and mathematical model to provide a revealing insight into the nitrogen removal mechanism in the IAMR. Metagenomics revealed that a complex nitrogen metabolic network, including aerobic ammonia and nitrite oxidation, anammox, denitrification via nitrate and nitrite, and nitrate respiration, existed in the IAMR. A novel method for solving kinetic parameters with high stability was developed based on a genetic algorithm. Use this method to calculate the kinetics of various reactions involved in nitrogen metabolism. Kinetics revealed that simultaneous partial nitritation-anammox (PN/A) and partial denitrification-anammox (PDN/A) were the dominant approaches to nitrogen removal in the IAMR. Finally, a kinetics-based model was proposed for quantitatively describing the nitrogen metabolic network under the limitation of COD. 58% ~ 67% of nitrogen was removed via the anammox-based processes (PN/A and PDN/A), but only 7% ~ 12% and 1% ~ 2% of nitrogen were removed via heterotrophic denitrification of nitrite and nitrate, respectively. The half-inhibition constant of dissolved oxygen (DO) on anammox was simulated as 0.37 ~ 0.60 mg L‚àí 1, filling the gap in quantifying DO inhibition on anammox. High-frequency intermittent aeration was identified as the crucial measure to suppress nitrite-oxidizing bacteria, although it has a high affinity for DO and NO2‚àí -N. In continuous aeration mode, the simulated NO3‚àí -N in the IAMR would rise by 39.6%. The research provides a novel insight into the nitrogen removal mechanism in single-stage microaerobic systems and provides a reliable approach to practicing PN/A and PDN/A for cost-effective nitrogen removal.

This paper addresses the following key problems:

  • Unquantified nitrogen metabolic network: The nitrogen metabolism in single-stage microaerobic processes treating low COD/TN wastewater is complex, involving combined kinetics of various reactions (aerobic ammonia oxidation, nitrite oxidation, anammox, denitrification, nitrate respiration), yet the nitrogen metabolic network had not been quantitatively described.

  • Lack of reliable kinetic parameter estimation methods: The conventional linear fitting algorithm based on the integration of the Monod equation is unreliable when measurement random error exceeds 0.5%, leading to large errors in estimating Œºmax\mu_{max}Œºmax‚Äã and KSK_SKS‚Äã.

Q: What are the points that can be explored further?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

  • Long-term stability of simultaneous PN/A and PDN/A: This study demonstrated co-existence of PN/A and PDN/A in a single-stage IAMR over 57 days, but long-term (>1 year) stability and resilience to influent fluctuations (COD/TN ratio, temperature, pH) remain to be investigated.

  • Precise determination of KDO,amK_{DO,am}KDO,am‚Äã: The DO half-inhibition constant of anammox was estimated indirectly via model calibration as KDO,amK_{DO,am}KDO,am‚Äã = 0.37‚Äì0.60 mg/L. Direct experimental measurement of KDO,amK_{DO,am}KDO,am‚Äã under controlled DO gradients would provide more accurate values and reduce model uncertainty.

  • Quantification of DNRA contribution: The paper found that DNRA (dissimilatory nitrate reduction to ammonia) genes were up-regulated, but no significant NH4+NH_4^+NH4+‚Äã-N accumulation was observed. The actual contribution of DNRA to the nitrogen cycle in the IAMR was not fully quantified and deserves further study.

  • Optimization of intermittent aeration strategy: The paper identified high-frequency intermittent aeration (18 min on / 6 min off) as crucial for NOB suppression, but the optimal aeration frequency, duration, and DO setpoint for maximizing PN/A + PDN/A efficiency while minimizing NO3‚àíNO_3^-NO3‚àí‚Äã-N accumulation have not been systematically optimized.

  • Scale-up and application to other low COD/TN wastewaters: The IAMR was tested only at lab scale with piggery wastewater. Application to other types of low COD/TN wastewater (e.g., landfill leachate, municipal wastewater sidestreams) and scale-up to pilot or full scale need to be explored.

  • Microbial community dynamics and functional redundancy: While metagenomics identified key functional genes, the dynamics of microbial community composition (e.g., AOB, NOB, anammox bacteria, denitrifiers) under varying operating conditions and their functional redundancy were not deeply analyzed.

  • Integration of PDN/A promotion strategies: Since PDN/A was found to reduce 84.6% of produced NO3‚àíNO_3^-NO3‚àí‚Äã-N, strategies to further enhance PDN/A activity (e.g., controlled addition of external carbon sources, optimization of anoxic/aerobic cycling) could be explored to further reduce NO3‚àíNO_3^-NO3‚àí‚Äã-N accumulation.

  • Model extension to include biomass growth and decay: The current kinetics-based model does not explicitly account for biomass growth, decay, and washout dynamics of different microbial groups (AOB, NOB, anammox, heterotrophs), which limits its predictive power for long-term operation and startup scenarios.

  • Effect of temperature on kinetic parameters: All kinetic assays were conducted at 32¬∞C. The temperature dependence of the Monod parameters (especially KDO,amK_{DO,am}KDO,am‚Äã) for the various reactions in the IAMR has not been characterized, which is important for applications in different climate zones.

  • Interaction between anammox and DNRA under COD-limited conditions: The paper showed no interaction between anammox and heterotrophic nitrite denitrification in the presence of COD, but the interaction under COD-limited conditions (as in the actual IAMR) was not directly tested.
