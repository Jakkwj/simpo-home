Phosphate release involving PAOs activity during anaerobic fermentation of EBPR sludge and the extension of ADM1

DOI: 10.1016/j.cej.2015.10.110

Authors: Ruyi Wang, Yongmei Li, Wenling Chen, Jinte Zou, Yinguang Chen

  • Abstract
  • Objective
  • Solution
  • Experiment
  • Exploration
  • Summary
  • Related

Anaerobic fermentation of the enhanced biological phosphorus removal (EBPR) sludge was investigated in terms of phosphate release and volatile fatty acids (VFAs) production regarding polyphosphate accumulating organisms (PAOs) activity. PAOs decay rate during fermentation was determined as 0.35 ¬± 0.03 d-1. Sludge lysis was enhanced with an increase in polyhydroxyalkanoate (PHA) content. Moreover, the phosphate release profiles and the VFAs production as well as the individual VFA fractions varied with different acetate concentrations added initially. Based on these observations, anaerobic digestion model No. 1 was extended and modified by introducing: (1) processes that PAOs store 4 VFA species as PHA, which can be degraded into varied fractions of individual VFA in dependence on PHA composition, (2) the effect of PHA content on disintegration rate, and (3) phosphorus precipitation. The proposed model adequately fitted a multi-experiment, multi-variable data set, indicating that it plays an important role in predicting phosphate and VFAs variations during EBPR sludge fermentation.

Q: What problem does this paper attempt to solve?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

  • Lack of understanding of phosphate release behavior during anaerobic fermentation of EBPR sludge, particularly regarding the activity of polyphosphate accumulating organisms (PAOs). While phosphorus release during anaerobic digestion has been studied, the specific mechanisms involving PAOs activity during fermentation were not well characterized.
  • Absence of a comprehensive mathematical model that incorporates phosphate release processes related to PAOs activity into the widely-used Anaerobic Digestion Model No. 1 (ADM1). Existing models (e.g., BNRM1, BNRM2, UCTADM1 extension) either oversimplify biological processes or do not account for: (1) phosphate release via PAOs-mediated PHA storage from 4 different VFA species, (2) the effect of PHA content on sludge disintegration rate, and (3) phosphorus precipitation (struvite and K-struvite).
  • Undesirable in-reactor phosphorus precipitation during anaerobic treatment of EBPR sludge, which limits phosphorus availability in the liquid phase and causes blockages in treatment facilities, hindering efficient phosphorus recovery.
  • Determination of PAOs decay rate under anaerobic fermentation conditions (35¬∞C), which was previously only reported under aerobic conditions at lower temperatures.
  • Understanding how initial acetate concentration affects PHA composition, VFA fractions produced from PHA lysis, and phosphate release profiles during EBPR sludge fermentation.

  • A reliable dynamic model is needed to predict phosphate and VFAs variations to optimize EBPR sludge treatment and enhance phosphorus recovery.

Q: What are the points that can be explored further?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

  • Extension and improvement of the precipitation module: The current model only considers MgNH4PO4MgNH_4PO_4MgNH4‚ÄãPO4‚Äã (struvite) and MgKPO4MgKPO_4MgKPO4‚Äã (K-struvite) precipitation. Probable dissolution processes and other species likely to precipitate (e.g., calcium phosphates, newberyite) under more complex conditions are not considered. Future work should expand the precipitation module to improve simulation of PO43‚àíPO_4^{3-}PO43‚àí‚Äã, Mg2+Mg^{2+}Mg2+, and K+K^+K+ dynamics.
  • Validation with diverse EBPR sludge types and operating conditions: The model was established and validated using a single set of experiments with a single kind of EBPR sludge. The model and its parameters should be further validated with sludge from different EBPR systems, different wastewater compositions, and different operating conditions (e.g., temperature, pH, SRT).
  • Scale-up from lab to full-scale: The parameter fdisf_{dis}fdis‚Äã (PHA effect on disintegration) was obtained from lab-scale experiments. Its value may need adjustment for full-scale anaerobic fermentation systems due to different operating conditions.
  • Inhibitory effect of high phosphate concentration on anaerobic digestion: The authors noted in a previous study that high orthophosphate concentration inhibits anaerobic digestion. This inhibition mechanism could be incorporated into the extended ADM1 model for more accurate simulation of EBPR sludge treatment.
  • Optimization of short-term fermentation strategy: The paper suggests that short-term fermentation with additional acetate can maximize phosphate release and minimize in-reactor precipitation. Further optimization of acetate dosage, fermentation time, and operating conditions for enhanced phosphorus recovery could be explored.
  • Microbial community dynamics: The model focuses on PAOs, but the interactions between PAOs and other microbial communities (e.g., glycogen accumulating organisms, GAOs) during anaerobic fermentation are not considered. Including GAO competition could improve model accuracy.
  • Integration into plant-wide models: The extended ADM1 could be integrated into plant-wide wastewater treatment models (e.g., BNRM2) to simulate the entire treatment line including phosphorus recovery from fermented sludge liquor.
  • Detailed PHA composition modeling: The current model uses lumped PHA fractions. A more detailed mechanistic description of PHB, PHV, and PH2MV synthesis and degradation pathways could improve prediction of individual VFA fractions.
  • Effect of temperature on model parameters: The decay rate and other kinetic parameters were determined at 35¬∞C. Temperature correction functions for these parameters should be developed for application at different operating temperatures.
