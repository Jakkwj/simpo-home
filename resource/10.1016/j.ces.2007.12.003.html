An approach for modeling two-step denitrification in activated sludge systems

DOI: 10.1016/j.ces.2007.12.003

Authors: Bing-Jie Ni, Han-Qing Yu

In the widely used Activated Sludge Models (ASMs) for biological wastewater treatment, denitrification is assumed as a single-step process, which is not true for some cases where a considerable amount of nitrite is build-up in system. This causes limitation to the application of ASMs. In this work, with a consideration of denitrification on both nitrite and nitrate, and an introduction of the simultaneous storage and growth concept, a two-step ASM3-based denitrification model is established. The sensitivity of the effluent chemical oxygen demand, NO2−–N and NO3−–N concentrations toward the stoichiometric and kinetic coefficients is analyzed. Model calibration is performed by comparing the measured and predicted values for model components. Thereafter, this model is evaluated with the experimental results of four different case studies on denitrification and compared with the original ASM3. The evaluation and comparison results demonstrate that the developed model is able to better and more mechanistically describe the denitrification process in the activated sludge systems.

Q: What problem does this paper attempt to solve?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

The paper addresses the following key problems:

  • Limitation of single-step denitrification assumption in ASMs: In the widely used Activated Sludge Models (ASM1, ASM3, etc.), denitrification is modeled as a single-step process (directly from NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​ to N2N_2N2​), completely neglecting nitrite (NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​) as an intermediate product.
  • Inability to describe nitrite accumulation: In certain conditions (e.g., suppressed denitrifier activity, limited carbon source, specific COD/N ratios, pH, and temperature), a considerable amount of NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​ can build up in the system. The original ASM3 cannot describe this phenomenon.
  • Lack of storage-growth coupling under anoxic conditions: The original ASM3 assumes all readily biodegradable substrate is first stored before being used for growth, which is inadequate for describing feast-famine behavior. The simultaneous storage and growth concept under anoxic conditions has been less studied compared to aerobic conditions.
  • Inaccurate NUR (Nitrate Utilization Rate) interpretation: When significant NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​ accumulation occurs, measuring only NOx−NO_x^-NOx−​-N or NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​-N does not yield acceptable results, leading to errors in experimental data interpretation.

Gemini 3.1 Pro:

  • The widely used Activated Sludge Models (ASMs), such as ASM3, assume denitrification is a single-step process (directly from NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​ to N2N_2N2​).
  • This assumption is invalid when there is a considerable build-up of nitrite (NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​) in the system, limiting the application of ASMs.
  • Existing models lack a mechanistic description of the simultaneous storage and growth phenomena of heterotrophic microorganisms under anoxic conditions.

Q: How the paper solves this problem?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

The paper solves the above problems through the following approaches:

  • Two-step denitrification framework: The model explicitly considers denitrification as a two-step sequential process:

    • Step 1: 12NO3−→12NO2−\frac{1}{2}NO_3^- \rightarrow \frac{1}{2}NO_2^-21​NO3−​→21​NO2−​ (nitrate reduction to nitrite)
    • Step 2: 13NO2−→16N2\frac{1}{3}NO_2^- \rightarrow \frac{1}{6}N_231​NO2−​→61​N2​ (nitrite reduction to nitrogen gas) Each denitrification process (storage, growth on SSS_SSS​, growth on XSTOX_{STO}XSTO​, endogenous respiration) is split into two parallel kinetic expressions — one using SNO3S_{NO_3}SNO3​​ and one using SNO2S_{NO_2}SNO2​​ as electron acceptors.

  • Introduction of simultaneous storage and growth concept: Based on the concept proposed by Karahan et al. (2006), the model allows heterotrophs to simultaneously store external substrate as internal polymers (XSTOX_{STO}XSTO​, e.g., PHB/glycogen) and grow directly on external substrate. Three independent yield coefficients are used: YSTO,DY_{STO,D}YSTO,D​ (storage), YHD,SY_{HD,S}YHD,S​ (direct growth on SSS_SSS​), and YHD,STOY_{HD,STO}YHD,STO​ (growth on XSTOX_{STO}XSTO​).

  • ASM3-based matrix model structure: The model is structured with 8 state variables (XHDX_{HD}XHD​, XSTOX_{STO}XSTO​, SSS_SSS​, XSX_SXS​, SN2S_{N_2}SN2​​, SNO3S_{NO_3}SNO3​​, SNO2S_{NO_2}SNO2​​, XIX_IXI​, SOS_OSO​) and 9 microbial processes, presented in a standard stoichiometric matrix format (Tables 1 and 2) compatible with the ASM framework.

  • Monod-type kinetics with switch functions: Growth and storage are described by Monod-type expressions. An inverse switch function (KSKS+SS\frac{K_S}{K_S + S_S}KS​+SS​KS​​) is incorporated to activate XSTOX_{STO}XSTO​ consumption only when external substrate SSS_SSS​ is depleted (famine phase).

  • Sensitivity analysis: A one-variable-at-a-time sensitivity analysis is performed to identify the most influential parameters (e.g., kSTO,Dk_{STO,D}kSTO,D​, μHD,STO\mu_{HD,STO}μHD,STO​, μHD,S\mu_{HD,S}μHD,S​, bHDb_{HD}bHD​, KSK_SKS​, KNO2K_{NO_2}KNO2​​, KNO3K_{NO_3}KNO3​​) for model calibration.

  • Model calibration using AQUASIM 2.0: The model is calibrated by minimizing the sum of squared deviations between measured and predicted values using the AQUASIM 2.0 software platform.

Gemini 3.1 Pro:

  • Develops a two-step ASM3-based denitrification model that explicitly includes both nitrate (NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​) and nitrite (NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​) as electron acceptors.
  • Incorporates the concept of simultaneous microbial storage and growth under anoxic conditions, dividing the process into feast and famine phases.
  • Formulates a stoichiometric matrix and kinetic rate expressions for nine microbial processes, including anoxic storage and growth on both external substrates and internal storage polymers.
  • Utilizes AQUASIM 2.0 software for model simulation, sensitivity analysis, and parameter calibration.
  • Validates the proposed model by comparing its predictions with experimental data from four independent case studies and benchmarking it against the original ASM3.

Q: What experiments were done for the paper?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

The paper does not conduct new original experiments. Instead, it uses experimental data from four published case studies for model evaluation and comparison with the original ASM3:

  • Case 1 — Cokgor et al. (1998):

    • Anoxic respirometric analysis of municipal wastewater treatment.
    • NUR (Nitrate Utilization Rate) measurements were carried out in anoxic batch reactors.
    • The readily biodegradable COD fraction in wastewater was assessed.
    • Significant NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​-N accumulation was observed during the experiment, making NOx−NO_x^-NOx−​-N or NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​-N measurement alone inaccurate.
    • The model was used to simulate NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​-N profiles with and without NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​-N correction.

  • Case 2 — Beun et al. (2000):

    • Storage and degradation of poly-β-hydroxybutyrate (PHB) under denitrifying conditions in activated sludge cultures.
    • An SBR (Sequencing Batch Reactor) with 2 L working volume, operated in 4-h cycles with 8-h hydraulic retention time.
    • Measured profiles: acetate, PHB, NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​-N, and NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​-N over one cycle.
    • Nitrite accumulation during the feast phase and subsequent consumption during famine phase were observed.

  • Case 3 — Oh and Silverstein (1999):

    • Nitrite accumulation in denitrifying activated sludge in SBRs under carbon/electron source (acetate) limitation.
    • Uniformly anoxic mixing, constant substrate and mineral addition, constant SRT and MLVSS.
    • Initial nitrate: 50 mg N L−1^{-1}−1; C/N = 1:1 (acetate-limited).
    • Measured profiles: NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​-N, NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​-N, and acetate (COD).

  • Case 4 — Buys et al. (2000):

    • Measurement of denitrifying activity of activated sludge by tracking N2N_2N2​ gas production (pressure and gas composition in headspace of sealed bottles).
    • Lab-scale reactor fed with synthetic influent: 1000 mg NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​-N L−1^{-1}−1, 1300 mg acetate-C L−1^{-1}−1 (COD/N = 3.5 g COD g−1^{-1}−1 N).
    • Two initial substrate concentrations tested: 100 mg N L−1^{-1}−1 and 1000 mg N L−1^{-1}−1.
    • N2N_2N2​ production profiles were simulated and compared with the developed model and plain ASM3.

In addition, sensitivity analysis was performed computationally (not experimentally) using AQUASIM 2.0, varying all kinetic and stoichiometric parameters ±50% from their nominal values to evaluate their influence on effluent COD, NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​-N, and NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​-N concentrations.

Gemini 3.1 Pro:

  • The paper primarily conducts computational modeling and simulation experiments rather than new wet-lab experiments.
  • Sensitivity Analysis: Evaluated the impact of stoichiometric and kinetic parameters (e.g., hydrolysis rate, yield coefficients, affinity constants) on model outputs (effluent COD, NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​-N, NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​-N).
  • Model Calibration and Evaluation: Used four sets of experimental data from previous literature to verify the model:
    1. Anoxic respirometric analysis on municipal wastewater (Cokgor et al., 1998).
    2. Storage and degradation of PHB under denitrifying conditions in a Sequencing Batch Reactor (SBR) (Beun et al., 2000).
    3. Nitrite accumulation in denitrifying activated sludge in an acetate-limited SBR (Oh and Silverstein, 1999).
    4. Denitrifying activity measurement via gas production tracking (Buys et al., 2000).

Q: What are the points that can be explored further?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

Based on the paper's content and limitations, the following points can be explored further:

  • Extension to full biological nitrogen removal (BNR) systems: The current model focuses only on denitrification under anoxic conditions. Integration with nitrification (two-step: ammonia oxidation to nitrite, then nitrite oxidation to nitrate) would enable a complete two-step nitrogen cycle model applicable to systems like SHARON-Anammox or partial nitritation processes.

  • Incorporation of aerobic conditions: The simultaneous storage and growth concept is well-studied under aerobic conditions, but the current model is limited to anoxic denitrification. A unified model covering both aerobic and anoxic phases (e.g., for SBR systems with alternating aerobic/anoxic cycles) would be more comprehensive.

  • Modeling of intermediate nitrogen species (NO, N2ON_2ON2​O): The denitrification pathway includes NO and N2ON_2ON2​O as intermediates (NO3−⇒NO2−⇒NO⇒0.5N2O⇒0.5N2NO_3^- \Rightarrow NO_2^- \Rightarrow NO \Rightarrow 0.5N_2O \Rightarrow 0.5N_2NO3−​⇒NO2−​⇒NO⇒0.5N2​O⇒0.5N2​). The current model only considers two steps (NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​ to NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​, and NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​ to N2N_2N2​). Modeling N2ON_2ON2​O emissions is increasingly important for greenhouse gas accounting in wastewater treatment.

  • Microbial community differentiation: The model uses a single group of denitrifiers (XHDX_{HD}XHD​) capable of reducing both nitrate and nitrite. In reality, different microbial populations may preferentially reduce nitrate or nitrite. Incorporating distinct microbial groups could improve mechanistic accuracy.

  • Dynamic parameter estimation under varying environmental conditions: The model parameters (e.g., kSTO,Dk_{STO,D}kSTO,D​, μHD,S\mu_{HD,S}μHD,S​, bHDb_{HD}bHD​) vary significantly across the four case studies (Table 4), suggesting strong dependence on wastewater characteristics, temperature, pH, and sludge type. Developing temperature- and pH-correction functions for these parameters would improve model generalizability.

  • Application to continuous flow systems: The model evaluation is primarily based on SBR batch data. Validation in continuous flow systems (e.g., plug-flow reactors, CSTRs) under steady-state and dynamic conditions would broaden its applicability.

  • Integration with phosphorus removal models (ASM2d): Combining the two-step denitrification concept with biological phosphorus removal (EBPR) processes, as in ASM2d, would be valuable for modeling systems with simultaneous N and P removal.

  • Uncertainty and identifiability analysis: While sensitivity analysis is performed, a formal uncertainty analysis and parameter identifiability study (e.g., using Monte Carlo methods or Fisher Information Matrix) would provide more rigorous confidence intervals for the calibrated parameters.

  • Real-time control applications: The model could be used as a basis for developing model-based control strategies for denitrification processes, particularly for systems prone to nitrite accumulation.

Gemini 3.1 Pro:

  • Application and validation of the two-step denitrification model in full-scale municipal and industrial wastewater treatment plants.
  • Integration of the proposed model with other biological nutrient removal (BNR) processes, such as enhanced biological phosphorus removal (EBPR).
  • Further investigation into the microbial community dynamics and how different species contribute to nitrite accumulation and polymer storage.
  • Refinement of the calibration for affinity constants (e.g., KSK_SKS​), which showed wide variation depending on wastewater characteristics and reactor operating conditions.

Q: Summarize the main points of the paper.

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

This paper presents the development, calibration, and evaluation of a two-step ASM3-based denitrification model for activated sludge systems. The main points are:

  1. Motivation: The standard ASMs (particularly ASM3) model denitrification as a single-step process (NO3−→N2NO_3^- \rightarrow N_2NO3−​→N2​), ignoring NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​ as an intermediate. This is inadequate when significant nitrite accumulation occurs in the system.

  2. Model structure: The proposed model includes:

    • 8 state variables: XHDX_{HD}XHD​, XSTOX_{STO}XSTO​, SSS_SSS​, XSX_SXS​, SN2S_{N_2}SN2​​, SNO3S_{NO_3}SNO3​​, SNO2S_{NO_2}SNO2​​, XIX_IXI​ (and SOS_OSO​)
    • 9 microbial processes: hydrolysis of XSX_SXS​; anoxic storage of SSS_SSS​ on SNO3S_{NO_3}SNO3​​ and SNO2S_{NO_2}SNO2​​; anoxic growth on SSS_SSS​ with SNO3S_{NO_3}SNO3​​ and SNO2S_{NO_2}SNO2​​; anoxic growth on XSTOX_{STO}XSTO​ with SNO3S_{NO_3}SNO3​​ and SNO2S_{NO_2}SNO2​​; anoxic endogenous respiration on SNO3S_{NO_3}SNO3​​ and SNO2S_{NO_2}SNO2​​
    • Presented in standard ASM stoichiometric matrix format (Tables 1 & 2)

  3. Simultaneous storage and growth: The model incorporates the concept of simultaneous substrate storage and growth under anoxic conditions, using three independent yield coefficients: YSTO,DY_{STO,D}YSTO,D​, YHD,SY_{HD,S}YHD,S​, and YHD,STOY_{HD,STO}YHD,STO​.

  4. Sensitivity analysis: A one-variable-at-a-time sensitivity analysis reveals that:

    • Parameters related to anoxic storage (kSTO,Dk_{STO,D}kSTO,D​), endogenous decay (bHDb_{HD}bHD​), and growth (μHD,S\mu_{HD,S}μHD,S​, μHD,STO\mu_{HD,STO}μHD,STO​) significantly affect NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​-N and NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​-N outputs.
    • Effluent COD is mainly sensitive to hydrolysis parameters (kHDk_{HD}kHD​, KXK_XKX​).
    • KNO2K_{NO_2}KNO2​​ and KNO3K_{NO_3}KNO3​​ have opposing effects on NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​-N and NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​-N outputs.

  5. Model calibration: Five key parameters (kSTO,Dk_{STO,D}kSTO,D​, μHD,STO\mu_{HD,STO}μHD,STO​, μHD,S\mu_{HD,S}μHD,S​, bHDb_{HD}bHD​, KSK_SKS​) are calibrated using AQUASIM 2.0 by minimizing the sum of squared deviations between measured and predicted values.

  6. Model evaluation with four case studies:

    • Case 1 (Cokgor et al., 1998): The model correctly simulates NUR data with NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​-N correction; ASM3 overestimates nitrate.
    • Case 2 (Beun et al., 2000): The model accurately simulates acetate, PHB, NO3−NO_3^-NO3−​-N, and NO2−NO_2^-NO2−​-N profiles in an SBR cycle, including nitrite accumulation during feast and consumption during famine; ASM3 fails.
    • Case 3 (Oh and Silverstein, 1999): The model simulates nitrite accumulation under acetate-limited conditions; ASM3 overestimates nitrate and acetate.
    • Case 4 (Buys et al., 2000): The model accurately predicts N2N_2N2​ gas production at two initial substrate concentrations; ASM3 underestimates N2N_2N2​ production.

  7. Conclusion: The developed two-step denitrification model outperforms the original ASM3 in describing nitrite accumulation, storage dynamics, and overall denitrification behavior in activated sludge systems.

Gemini 3.1 Pro:

  • A novel two-step denitrification model based on ASM3 was successfully developed to address the limitations of single-step denitrification assumptions.
  • The model mechanistically incorporates simultaneous storage and growth concepts under anoxic conditions, utilizing both nitrate and nitrite as electron acceptors.
  • Sensitivity analysis indicated that parameters related to anoxic storage, endogenous decay, and growth on storage polymers significantly influence the effluent nitrogen concentrations.
  • Model evaluation using four different experimental case studies demonstrated that the developed model accurately predicts nitrite accumulation and storage processes, outperforming the original ASM3.
