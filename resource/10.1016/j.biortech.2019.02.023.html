Competitive dynamics of anaerobes during long-term biological sulfate reduction process in a UASB reactor

DOI: 10.1016/j.biortech.2019.02.023

Authors: Hong Chen, Jiang Wu, Bing Liu, Yu-you Li, Hidenari Yasui

  • Abstract
  • Objective
  • Solution
  • Experiment
  • Exploration
  • Summary
  • Related

To reveal the long-term competitive dynamics of anaerobes in anaerobic bioreactors with sulfate reduction, a comprehensive structured mathematical model was designed for an extension of the Anaerobic Digestion Model No. 1 (ADM1). Sulfate reduction bacteria (SRB) were categorized into acetogenic-likewise SRB (ASRB) and methanogenic-likewise SRB (MSRB). Experimental data from 329 days of continuous operation of a laboratory-scale upflow anaerobic sludge bed (UASB) reactor was used for model calibration and validation. Results show that the model has a good agreement with experimental data and that three stages including the MPA dominant, stalemate and SRB dominant stages were clearly appeared throughout the whole competition period. The model was capable of predicting the long-term dynamic competition of sulfidogens and methanogens for electrons. This could explain a long-term of over 200 days needed for the SRB out-competing the MPA, and support speculation that the SRB could finally out-compete both the AcB and the MPA.

Q: What problem does this paper attempt to solve?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

  • Long-term competitive dynamics of anaerobes in sulfate-rich anaerobic bioreactors: The competition between sulfate-reducing bacteria (SRB) and methane-producing archaea (MPA) has been studied for over three decades, yet the long-term dynamic mechanisms remain poorly understood. Specifically, it was unclear why SRB required over 200 days to out-compete MPA in UASB reactors.
  • Lack of a comprehensive mathematical model: Existing ADM1-based models either did not include sulfate reduction processes, or were validated only over short operation periods (e.g., 77 days), making them insufficient to describe long-term competitive dynamics.
  • Categorization of SRB functional groups: Previous models did not adequately distinguish between different functional types of SRB. This paper categorizes SRB into acetogenic-likewise SRB (ASRB) and methanogenic-likewise SRB (MSRB) to better reflect their distinct metabolic roles in competition with different anaerobic groups (EAcB, AMPA, HMPA).
  • Explaining the "stalemate stage": The existence of a prolonged transitional "stalemate stage" between MPA dominance and SRB dominance had not been mechanistically explained or modeled before.
  • Thermodynamic speculation validation: From a thermodynamic perspective, SRB should be able to out-compete both MPA and acetogenic bacteria (AcB), but this had not been confirmed by long-term dynamic modeling.

Q: What are the points that can be explored further?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

  • Improvement of MSRB population dynamics modeling: The model showed only small changes in MSRB population, which was difficult to explain by either experimental evidence or fundamental theories. Further research is needed to better understand and model the metabolic characteristics and population dynamics of MSRB.
  • Refinement of inhibition mechanisms: The paper used a simplified non-competitive inhibition function for free H2SH_2SH2‚ÄãS. More sophisticated inhibition models (e.g., competitive inhibition, threshold-based inhibition) could be explored to improve accuracy, especially for the MPA dominant stage where deviations were largest.
  • Substrate threshold concentration for Monod kinetics: A substrate threshold concentration for Monod kinetic modification could be reasonably used for modeling the competition for hydrogen between sulfate reducers and methanogens, as suggested by Ribes et al. (2004).
  • More accurate classification and optimization of SRB model structure: With further understanding of the metabolic characteristics and biological pathways of SRB, the model structure could be more accurately classified and optimized (e.g., distinguishing more SRB functional groups).
  • Application to different COD/sulfate ratios: The current model was validated only at COD/sulfate = 1. Exploring model performance under varying COD/sulfate ratios would broaden its applicability.
  • Integration with more complex wastewater compositions: The model could be extended to treat real industrial wastewaters with more complex compositions (e.g., simultaneous carbon-nitrogen-sulfur removal).
  • Granule-scale modeling: The current model treats the UASB as a completely mixed reactor. A multi-scale approach considering granule structure and mass transfer limitations could improve predictions.
  • Long-term validation beyond 329 days: The model predictions beyond the experimental period (days 329‚Äì450) were not validated. Longer-term experimental data would be valuable.
  • Homoacetogenesis inclusion: Homoacetogenesis was excluded in this model due to its low proportion. Under different operating conditions, its contribution may be more significant and should be considered.
  • Accurate determination of anaerobic population dynamics: More precise microbial community analysis methods (e.g., quantitative PCR, metagenomics) could provide better experimental data to support and validate model predictions of population dynamics.

  • Further understanding of the metabolic characteristics, inhibition mechanisms, and biological pathways of SRB to refine the mathematical models.
