Development of a kinetic model to evaluate thiosulfate-driven denitrification and anammox (TDDA) process

DOI: 10.1016/j.watres.2021.117155

Authors: Yang-Fan Deng, Wen-Tao Tang, Hao Huang, Jin Qian, Di Wu, Guang-Hao Chen

Recently, the integration of sulfur-driven denitrification and anammox process has been extensively studied as a promising alternative nitrogen removal technology. Most of these studies investigated the process feasibility and monitored the community dynamics. However, an in-depth understanding of this new sulfur‚Äìnitrogen cycle bioprocess based on mathematical modeling and elucidation of complex interactions among different microorganisms has not yet been achieved. To fill this gap, we developed a kinetic model (with 7 bioprocesses, 12 variables, and 19 parameters) to assess the sulfur(thiosulfate)-driven denitrification and anammox (TDDA) process in a single reactor. The parameters used in this process were separately estimated by fitting the data obtained from the experiments. Then, the model was further validated under different conditions, and the results demonstrated that the developed model could describe the dynamic behaviors of nitrogen and sulfur conversions in the TDDA system. The newly developed branched thiosulfate oxidation model was also verified by conducting a metagenomics analysis. Using the developed model, we i) examined the interactions between sulfur-oxidizing bacteria and anammox bacteria at steady-state conditions with varying substrates to demonstrate the reliability of TDDA, and ii) evaluated the feasibility and operation of the TDDA process in terms of practical implementation. Our results will benefit further exploration of the significance of this novel S-N cycle bioprocess and guide its future applications.

Q: What problem does this paper attempt to solve?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

  • Lack of mathematical model for the integrated TDDA system: Although the integration of sulfur-driven denitrification and anammox has been studied as a promising nitrogen removal technology, no kinetic mathematical model existed for the combined thiosulfate-driven denitrification and anammox (TDDA) process in a single reactor.
  • Incomplete understanding of thiosulfate oxidation pathways: Previous simplified models assumed thiosulfate was directly oxidized to sulfate without intermediates (Sox pathway), but in the TDDA system, significant elemental sulfur (S0S^0S0) accumulation was observed, indicating a different (branched) oxidation pathway that had not been modeled.
  • Unknown interactions between SOB and anammox bacteria: The complex interactions between sulfur-oxidizing bacteria (SOB) and anammox bacteria ‚Äî including competition for NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã as electron acceptor, and the supply of NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã from partial denitrification to anammox ‚Äî were not quantitatively understood.
  • No practical tool for process evaluation and optimization: There was no model-based tool to assess the feasibility of the TDDA process, evaluate the effects of key operational parameters (e.g., S/N ratio, NH4+/NO3‚àíNH_4^+/NO_3^-NH4+‚Äã/NO3‚àí‚Äã ratio), and guide thiosulfate dosage for treating industrial wastewaters containing high concentrations of nitrate and ammonium.

Q: What are the points that can be explored further?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

  • Genome-centric metagenomics: The paper notes that binning scaffolds from metagenomic assembly to reconstruct genomes of individual species needs to be further explored. This genome-centric approach would provide a better understanding of the phylogenetic diversity and metabolic pathways in the TDDA system.

  • Discrepancy between batch and continuous reactor biomass yield coefficients: The different values of YSOB1Y_{SOB1}YSOB1‚Äã and YSOB2Y_{SOB2}YSOB2‚Äã between batch and continuous reactor conditions remained unclear. The potential role of chemical or biological oxidation of thiosulfate by other electron acceptors (e.g., oxygen) in continuous reactors warrants further investigation.

  • Inhibitory effects of nitrite: The model did not consider the inhibitory effects of NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã on denitrification and anammox, as the maximum nitrite concentration in the experiments was 40.0 mgN/L at pH 8.0. Future studies could incorporate nitrite inhibition kinetics for systems with higher nitrite concentrations.

  • NO and N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO intermediates in denitrification: The model simplified denitrification as a two-step process (NO3‚àí‚ÜíNO2‚àí‚ÜíN2NO_3^- \rightarrow NO_2^- \rightarrow N_2NO3‚àí‚Äã‚ÜíNO2‚àí‚Äã‚ÜíN2‚Äã) without considering NO and N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO intermediates. Incorporating these intermediates could improve model accuracy and is relevant for greenhouse gas emission assessment.

  • Coupling with heterotrophic denitrification: The paper briefly discusses the challenges of coupling heterotrophic denitrification with anammox (risk of heterotrophic overgrowth). A more comprehensive model incorporating heterotrophic denitrification could be developed for treating wastewaters containing both organics and inorganic nitrogen.

  • Scale-up and full-scale application: The model was developed and validated at lab scale. Its applicability to pilot- and full-scale TDDA systems under varying environmental conditions (temperature, pH, fluctuating influent quality) needs to be evaluated.

  • Extension to other sulfur electron donors: The current model focuses on thiosulfate (S2O32‚àíS_2O_3^{2-}S2‚ÄãO32‚àí‚Äã) and elemental sulfur (S0S^0S0). Extending the model to include other sulfur compounds (e.g., S2‚àíS^{2-}S2‚àí, SO32‚àíSO_3^{2-}SO32‚àí‚Äã, S4O62‚àíS_4O_6^{2-}S4‚ÄãO62‚àí‚Äã) would broaden its applicability.

  • Dynamic optimization of thiosulfate dosage: While the model provides a framework for evaluating S/N and NH4+/NO3‚àíNH_4^+/NO_3^-NH4+‚Äã/NO3‚àí‚Äã ratios, real-time model-based control strategies for thiosulfate dosage optimization in response to fluctuating wastewater compositions could be developed.

  • Biofilm and granular sludge modeling: The current model is based on suspended growth kinetics. Extending it to biofilm or granular sludge configurations (e.g., UASB with granular sludge) would require incorporating mass transfer limitations and spatial gradients.
