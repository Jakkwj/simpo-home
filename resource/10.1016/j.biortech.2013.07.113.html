Sulfate-reduction, sulfide-oxidation and elemental sulfur bioreduction process: Modeling and experimental validation

DOI: 10.1016/j.biortech.2013.07.113

Authors: Xijun Xu, Chuan Chen, Duu-Jong Lee, Aijie Wang, Wanqian Guo, Xu Zhou, Hongliang Guo, Ye Yuan, Nanqi Ren, Jo-Shu Chang

  • Solution

This study describes the sulfate-reducing (SR) and sulfide-oxidizing (SO) process using Monod-type model with best-fit model parameters both being reported and estimated. The molar ratio of oxygen to sulfide (ROS) significantly affects the kinetics of the SR + SO process. The S0 is produced by SO step but is later consumed by sulfur-reducing bacteria to lead to ‚Äò‚Äòrebound‚Äô‚Äô in sulfide concentration. The model correlated well all experimental data in the present SR + SO tests and the validity of this approach was confirmed by independent sulfur bioreduction tests in four denitrifying sulfide removal (DSR) systems. Modeling results confirm that the ratio of oxygen to sulfide is a key factor for controlling S0 formation and its bioreduction. Overlooking S0 bioreduction step would overestimate the yield of S0.

Q: What problem does this paper attempt to solve?

Gemini 3.0 Pro:

The paper attempts to solve the following problems in the context of treating sulfate-bearing wastewaters:

  • Modeling the combined process: Developing a mathematical model for the simultaneous sulfate-reducing (SR) and sulfide-oxidizing (SO) processes under microaerophilic conditions.
  • Addressing 'Sulfide Rebound': Explaining and modeling the phenomenon where sulfide concentration increases ('rebounds') after the depletion of oxygen, which is attributed to the bioreduction of elemental sulfur (S0S^0S0).
  • Correcting Sulfur Yield Estimation: Addressing the issue that overlooking the S0S^0S0 bioreduction step leads to an overestimation of the elemental sulfur yield in SR + SO systems.

Q: How the paper solves this problem?

The paper solves these problems by:

  • Developing a Kinetic Model: Constructing a Monod-type kinetic model that incorporates four main processes: sulfate reduction to sulfide, sulfide oxidation to elemental sulfur, elemental sulfur bioreduction to sulfide, and aerobic COD oxidation.

Q: What are the points that can be explored further?

Gemini 3.0 Pro:

  • Microbial Community Identification: While the paper suggests methanogenic bacteria might be responsible for sulfur bioreduction (citing Zhou et al., 2011), specific identification and characterization of the microbial community responsible for this step in the specific SR+SO system could be explored further.
  • Continuous Flow Systems: The current model was validated using batch tests. Applying and validating this model in continuous flow reactors (CSTR or biofilm reactors) would be a logical next step.
  • Mechanism of Inhibition: Further detailed study on the specific inhibition mechanisms of oxygen on the sulfur bioreduction process beyond the Monod-type inhibition term used.

Q: Summarize the main points of the paper.

Gemini 3.0 Pro:

  • Integrated Model: A comprehensive model including sulfate reduction, sulfide oxidation, and sulfur bioreduction was successfully developed and validated.
  • Impact of ROSR_{OS}ROS‚Äã: The molar ratio of oxygen to sulfide (ROSR_{OS}ROS‚Äã) is a critical factor controlling the kinetics and the final yield of elemental sulfur (S0S^0S0).
  • Sulfide Rebound: The phenomenon of sulfide concentration increasing after oxygen depletion is caused by the bioreduction of formed S0S^0S0 back to sulfide.
