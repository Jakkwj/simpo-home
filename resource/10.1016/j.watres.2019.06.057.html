Strategies for mitigating nitrous oxide production and decreasing the carbon footprint of a full-scale combined nitrogen and phosphorus removal activated sludge system

DOI: 10.1016/j.watres.2019.06.057

Authors: Ewa Zaborowska, Xi Lu, Jacek Makinia

  • Abstract
  • Objective
  • Solution
  • Experiment
  • Exploration
  • Summary
  • Related

Nitrous oxide (N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO) emitted from biological nutrient removal activated sludge systems contributes significantly to the total carbon footprint of modern wastewater treatment plants. In the present study, N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production and emissions were experimentally determined in a large-scale plant (220,000‚ÄØPE) employing combined nitrogen (N) and phosphorus (P) removal. As a modelling tool, the Activated Sludge Model 2d (ASM2d) was extended with modules describing multiple N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production pathways and N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO liquid-gas transfers. The new model was calibrated and validated using the results of laboratory experiments and full-scale measurements. Different operational strategies were evaluated following the proposed model-based procedure. Heterotrophic denitrification was found to be the predominant pathway of N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production under both anoxic and aerobic conditions. This behaviour could primarily be attributed to the predominant abundance of heterotrophic denitrifiers over nitrifiers. Simulations revealed that the optimal solution for minimizing liquid N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production is to set the dissolved oxygen concentration in the aerobic zone from 1 to 2‚ÄØmg O2/L and to enhance the mixed liquor recirculation rate (MLR) (>500% of the influent flowrate) while not compromising effluent standards. Regarding the actual conditions, the potential reduction in the carbon footprint was estimated to be 10% by applying the proposed operational strategy. The results suggest that considerable improvements can be achieved without substantial upgrades and increased costs.

Q: What problem does this paper attempt to solve?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

  • N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emissions quantification and mitigation in full-scale BNR systems: Nitrous oxide (N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO) is a potent greenhouse gas (GHG) with a global warming potential ~300 times that of CO2CO_2CO2‚Äã. Wastewater treatment plants (WWTPs) are significant sources of N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO, yet reliable prediction and mitigation strategies for full-scale combined biological nitrogen and phosphorus removal (BNR) systems remain limited.

  • Lack of validated N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO models for full-scale A2OA^2OA2O-type systems: Existing N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO models (e.g., Liu et al. (2015), Massara et al. (2018)) were either calibrated only on lab-scale enriched cultures or not validated against real full-scale data, making them inadequate for practical application in combined N and P removal systems.

  • Carbon footprint reduction: The paper seeks to identify operational strategies (manipulating dissolved oxygen (DO) concentration and mixed liquor recirculation (MLR) ratio) that can simultaneously reduce N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emissions, energy consumption, and the overall carbon footprint of a full-scale WWTP without compromising effluent quality standards.

  • Complexity of N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production pathways in systems with DPAOs: In systems with denitrifying polyphosphate accumulating organisms (DPAOs), the relative contributions of different microbial groups (AOB, OHOs, DPAOs) to N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production are unclear, complicating model development and process optimization.

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

The paper addresses these problems through a structured measurement‚Äìmodeling‚Äìanalysis framework:

1. Development of a Novel Biokinetic Model (ASM2d-N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO)‚Äã

  • An extended ASM2d model incorporating N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production and liquid-gas transfer was developed, implemented in GPS-X v7.0 using the Petersen-Gujer matrix notation.
  • The model includes:
    • Three AOB-mediated reactions: (i) NH4+NH_4^+NH4+‚Äã oxidation to N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO (hydroxylamine pathway), (ii) NH4+NH_4^+NH4+‚Äã oxidation to NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã (nitritation), (iii) NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã reduction to N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO (AOB denitrification).
    • Three OHO (heterotrophic) denitrification reactions: (i) NO3‚àíNO_3^-NO3‚àí‚Äã ‚Üí NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã, (ii) NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã ‚Üí N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO, (iii) N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO ‚Üí N2N_2N2‚Äã.
    • DPAOs were assumed to reduce NO3‚àíNO_3^-NO3‚àí‚Äã only to NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã (following Wisniewski et al. (2018)), with negligible N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production contribution.
    • A new state variable "N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO gas" (CN2OC_{N_2O}CN2‚ÄãO‚Äã) was added to describe liquid-gas transfer.
    • N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emission rates in aerobic zones were calculated using an empirical kLakLakLa model (Foley et al., 2010); anoxic zones used a fixed kLaN2O,processanoxic=2¬†d‚àí1kLa_{N_2O,process}^{anoxic} = 2\ d^{-1}kLaN2‚ÄãO,processanoxic‚Äã=2¬†d‚àí1.

2. Two-Step Calibration and Validation Procedure‚Äã

  • Trial no. 1: Laboratory-scale batch experiments (anaerobic-anoxic, anaerobic-aerobic, one-phase anoxic) and first full-scale measurement campaign were used to calibrate the base ASM2d parameters (without N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO data).
  • Trial no. 2: Laboratory-scale experiments with low DO (0.5 mg O2O_2O2‚Äã/L) and second full-scale measurement campaign (including liquid and gaseous N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO measurements) were used for model recalibration and validation with N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO data.
  • Sensitivity analysis (one-variable-at-a-time, normalized sensitivity coefficient Si,jS_{i,j}Si,j‚Äã) and parameter estimation (Nelder-Mead simplex method, maximum likelihood objective function) were performed in GPS-X.
  • 15 parameters were adjusted, including Œ∑NO3,H\eta_{NO3,H}Œ∑NO3,H‚Äã, Œ∑NO2,H\eta_{NO2,H}Œ∑NO2,H‚Äã, Œ∑N2O,H\eta_{N2O,H}Œ∑N2O,H‚Äã, KO,HK_{O,H}KO,H‚Äã, qPHAq_{PHA}qPHA‚Äã, qPPq_{PP}qPP‚Äã, ŒºAOB\mu_{AOB}ŒºAOB‚Äã, Œ∑1,AOB\eta_{1,AOB}Œ∑1,AOB‚Äã, Œ∑2,AOB\eta_{2,AOB}Œ∑2,AOB‚Äã, etc.

3. Steady-State Simulations for Mitigation Strategy Analysis‚Äã

  • Using routine summer 2016 operational data from the Slupsk WWTP (A2OA^2OA2O configuration, Poland) as reference, steady-state simulations were performed by varying:

    • MLR (mixed liquor recirculation) ratio

Q: What are the points that can be explored further?

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

  • Role of DPAOs in N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production: The paper simplifies DPAO contribution to N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO as negligible. However, under certain conditions (e.g., different carbon sources, varying electron acceptor availability), DPAOs may contribute significantly. More detailed experimental and modeling work on DPAO-mediated N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production (as in Liu et al. (2015) and Wisniewski et al. (2018)) is needed.

  • Winter/seasonal operational conditions: The optimization was based on summer 2016 data (T ‚âà 20¬∞C). The effect of lower temperatures on N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production pathways, model parameters (Arrhenius coefficients), and optimal DO/MLR settings should be investigated for year-round applicability.

  • Dynamic real-time control strategies: The paper uses steady-state simulations for optimization. Implementing dynamic (real-time) control of DO and MLR based on online N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO sensors could further reduce emissions and improve energy efficiency.

  • N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production under transient/dynamic loading conditions: The full-scale WWTP experiences significant diurnal and seasonal flow/load variations. Dynamic simulation-based optimization under varying influent conditions would provide more robust mitigation strategies.

  • Integration with plant-wide GHG accounting: The paper focuses on direct N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emissions and aeration/pumping energy. A comprehensive plant-wide carbon footprint analysis including sludge treatment, methane (CH4CH_4CH4‚Äã) emissions, and chemical usage would give a more complete picture.

  • Validation at other WWTP configurations: The ASM2d-N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO model was developed and validated only for the modified Bardenpho (A2OA^2OA2O) configuration at Slupsk WWTP. Its applicability to other configurations (e.g., step-feed, SBR, MBR, Carrousel) needs to be tested.

  • NOB suppression strategies: The paper mentions that enhancing NOB activity can reduce N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO but at the cost of effluent quality (Boiocchi et al., 2016). Partial nitritation/anammox or NOB suppression strategies combined with N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO mitigation deserve further exploration.

  • Uncertainty and sensitivity of N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO-related parameters: The paper identifies Œ∑NO2,H\eta_{NO2,H}Œ∑NO2,H‚Äã, Œ∑N2O,H\eta_{N2O,H}Œ∑N2O,H‚Äã, KNO2,HK_{NO2,H}KNO2,H‚Äã, and KN2O,HK_{N2O,H}KN2O,H‚Äã as (very) influential to N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO predictions. More rigorous uncertainty analysis and identifiability studies for these parameters are needed.

  • Online N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO monitoring integration: The use of N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO microsensors for model calibration is promising but requires further development of robust, low-maintenance online sensors suitable for long-term full-scale deployment.

  • Economic analysis of mitigation strategies: A detailed cost-benefit analysis comparing the capital and operational costs of implementing DO/MLR optimization versus the benefits of reduced GHG emissions and energy savings would strengthen the practical case for adoption.

Gemini 3.1 Pro:

  • The contribution of denitrifying polyphosphate accumulating organisms (DPAOs) to N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production, which was neglected in this study.\n- Long-term dynamic validation of the model under varying seasonal conditions (e.g., winter temperatures).\n- Application and validation of the ASM2d-N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO model in other full-scale WWTP configurations.

Q: Summarize the main points of the paper.

Claude Sonnet 4.6:

This paper presents a comprehensive measurement‚Äìmodeling‚Äìanalysis study on N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production, emission, and mitigation strategies at a full-scale combined nitrogen and phosphorus removal activated sludge system (modified Bardenpho/A2OA^2OA2O configuration) at the Slupsk WWTP in Poland. The main points are:

1. Development of ASM2d-N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO Model‚Äã

  • A novel biokinetic model (ASM2d-N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO) was developed by extending ASM2d to include N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production by AOB (hydroxylamine oxidation and AOB denitrification) and heterotrophic OHOs (incomplete denitrification via NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã and N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO as intermediates), as well as liquid-gas N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO transfer.
  • DPAOs were assumed to reduce NO3‚àíNO_3^-NO3‚àí‚Äã only to NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã with negligible N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production, simplifying the model structure.
  • The model was implemented in GPS-X v7.0 using the Petersen-Gujer matrix notation.

2. Two-Step Calibration and Validation‚Äã

  • A rigorous two-step calibration procedure was applied: first calibrating base ASM2d parameters (without N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO data) using lab-scale batch experiments and the first full-scale campaign, then recalibrating N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO-related parameters using low-DO lab experiments and validating against the second full-scale campaign (including liquid and gaseous N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO measurements).
  • The model achieved good agreement with measured data: r=0.83‚Äì0.91r = 0.83‚Äì0.91r=0.83‚Äì0.91 for NH4+NH_4^+NH4+‚Äã-N, r=0.64r = 0.64r=0.64 for PO43‚àíPO_4^{3-}PO43‚àí‚Äã-P, and r=0.91r = 0.91r=0.91 for NO3‚àíNO_3^-NO3‚àí‚Äã-N in the aerobic zones.
  • The reference state N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emission factor was 1.6% of TN load, consistent with literature values for similar systems.

3. Identification of Predominant N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO Production Pathway‚Äã

  • Heterotrophic denitrification (OHO-mediated incomplete denitrification, particularly NO2‚àíNO_2^-NO2‚àí‚Äã ‚Üí N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO) was identified as the predominant N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production pathway in this full-scale BNR system, rather than AOB-mediated pathways.

4. Mitigation Strategy Analysis via Steady-State Simulations‚Äã

  • Steady-state simulations varying DO concentration (in aerobic zones) and MLR ratio were performed using summer 2016 routine operational data as the reference.
  • The optimal operating conditions were identified as DO = 1.3 mg O2O_2O2‚Äã/L and MLR = 730% of influent flow.
  • At the optimum: N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emission reduced by ~11%, energy demand reduced, and total carbon footprint reduced by ~10%, without violating EU effluent standards (TN ‚â§ 10 mg N/L, TP ‚â§ 1 mg P/L).

5. Carbon Footprint Analysis‚Äã

  • The total carbon footprint was calculated as CO2CO_2CO2‚Äã equivalent emissions, combining direct N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emissions (converted using GWP factor) and indirect emissions from energy consumption (aeration blowers and pumps).
  • The study demonstrated that simultaneous reduction of N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emissions and energy consumption is achievable through operational optimization, providing a practical pathway to reduce the carbon footprint of full-scale WWTPs.

Gemini 3.1 Pro:

  • An ASM2d-N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO model was successfully developed and validated to predict liquid and gaseous N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO in full-scale bioreactors.\n- Heterotrophic denitrification was identified as the predominant N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production pathway.\n- Adjusting the DO concentration (to 1.3 mg O2O_2O2‚Äã/L) and MLR ratio (to 730%) effectively mitigated N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO production.\n- The optimized strategy led to an 11% reduction in N2ON_2ON2‚ÄãO emissions and a potential 10% reduction in the total carbon footprint without compromising effluent standards.
