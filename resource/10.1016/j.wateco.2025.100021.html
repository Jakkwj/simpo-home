The expansion of knowledge of and demand for wastewater treatment has driven the increasing complexity of its models. The establishment or use of such complicated models is difficult for environmental engineers, researchers and students, which hinders model development, knowledge transformation, and practical use of the wastewater treatment process. Even for reviewers and readers, it is difficult to evaluate whether a complicated model proposed in an article is reliable without time-consuming coding work. Thus, a new wastewater treatment modelling tool that is easy to use, code free, powerful, and open for sharing is necessary. To simplify wastewater treatment modelling and enhance accessibility, this study developed a Simplified Intelligent Modelling Platform Online for wastewater process modelling (SIMPO, https://www.simpowater.org), an open-source and free-of-charge platform. SIMPO introduces the following innovative features: 1) Intuitive drag-and-drop graphical user interfaces enable code-free modelling. 2) Dynamic adaptive algorithms (forward/improved Euler methods) optimize step size in real time to ensure computational accuracy and efficiency. 3) Balance checking of chemical stoichiometric matrix, automatic visualization of results, and Nash Sutcliffe efficiency (NSE)-based goodness-of-fit evaluation can help researchers identify subtle but impactful errors. 4) Advanced algorithms for sensitivity analysis, uncertainty assessment, and parameter estimation help researchers identify the importance of ranking parameters, understand the transmission of input uncertainty to output, and achieve a balance between parameter estimation time and accuracy in complex scenarios. 5) Open and shareable mathematical models, modification processes and calculation results promote knowledge sharing and exchange. The combination of SIMPO with artificial intelligence (AI) tools helps researchers understand and apply mathematical model papers. Validated against classic modelling tools, such as AQUASIM 2.1 developed by EAWAG, Zurich, Switzerland, SIMPO achieves consistent accuracy while significantly reducing barriers to its use. The calculation result of a classic case is consistent with AQUASIM and with weighted Nash Sutcliffe efficiency (WNSE)>0.999. Case studies demonstrate its capability to detect and resolve flaws in published models (eg, stoichiometric matrix imbalances in an extended model of Activated Sludge Model 3 and poor fitting of some variables in an autotrophic denitrification model). By combining powerful algorithms with a user-centric design, SIMPO provides an efficient, reliable and open solution for wastewater treatment modelling, driving innovation and accessibility in environmental engineering research. Currently, the use of large language model (LLM) tools such as KIMI can help users understand water quality modelling more quickly. In the future, application programming interface (API) integration with more AI tools such as DeepSeek would enable the possibility of linking classic mechanism-driven modelling with AI-assisted data-driven modelling.

Q: What problem does this paper attempt to solve?

Claude-4.5-Opus-Thinking: This paper addresses several critical challenges in wastewater treatment process modelling:

  1. Increasing model complexity: Modern wastewater treatment models have become exponentially complex with larger stoichiometric matrices, more parameters, and higher computational requirements, creating significant barriers for researchers and engineers.

  2. High technical barriers: Existing modelling tools require programming knowledge and advanced software expertise, making them inaccessible to many environmental engineers, researchers, and students.

  3. Difficulty in model verification: For reviewers and readers, it is challenging to evaluate whether complicated models proposed in published papers are reliable without time-consuming coding work.

  4. Lack of collaboration tools: Traditional platforms lack mechanisms for sharing models, data, and results, hindering knowledge transformation and practical application of wastewater treatment models.

  5. Limited error-detection capabilities: Subtle errors in model matrices (e.g., stoichiometric imbalances) are difficult to identify using conventional tools.

Gemini 3.0 Pro: The paper addresses the increasing complexity of wastewater treatment models (such as extended ASM models) and the significant barriers researchers, engineers, and students face in using them. Specific problems include:

  1. High Barrier to Entry: Traditional modeling software (e.g., AQUASIM) requires time-consuming coding or complex inputs, making it difficult for non-experts.
  2. Error-Prone Processes: Manual input of large stoichiometric matrices often leads to subtle but impactful errors.
  3. Lack of Shareability: It is difficult to share models, data, and results for peer review or collaboration, hindering knowledge transfer.
  4. Computational Difficulty: Advanced evaluations like sensitivity analysis and uncertainty assessment often require custom programming in traditional tools.

Q: How the paper solves this problem?

Claude-4.5-Opus-Thinking: The paper introduces SIMPO (Simplified Intelligent Modelling Platform Online), a next-generation cloud-based platform with the following solutions:

1. Code-free Modelling with Intuitive GUI‚Äã

  • Drag-and-drop graphical user interface for process design
  • Visual input assistance with color-coded components and parameters
  • ASM-style matrix editing interface familiar to wastewater professionals

2. Dynamic Adaptive Algorithms‚Äã

  • Forward and Improved Euler methods with automatic step size adjustment
  • Real-time optimization balancing computational accuracy and efficiency
  • Response strategies for handling negative concentration values

3. Automatic Error-proofing Mechanisms‚Äã

  • Balance checking: Verifies ThOD (Theoretical Oxygen Demand) and nitrogen balance in stoichiometric matrices
  • NSE-based evaluation: Provides Nash-Sutcliffe Efficiency for each variable to identify poorly fitted components
  • Automatic visualization of results to expose hidden problems

4. Advanced Evaluation Algorithms‚Äã

  • Sensitivity Analysis: OAT (One at A Time) algorithms with correlation analysis
  • Uncertainty Assessment: Monte Carlo procedure with Latin Hypercube Sampling (LHS)
  • Parameter Estimation: Genetic Algorithm (GA) with escape mechanism for efficient optimization

5. Open and Collaborative Platform‚Äã

  • Public/private repositories for models, data, and results
  • Version-controlled sharing with easy link switching
  • Integration with AI tools (KIMI) for paper comprehension
  • Free-of-charge SaaS model

Gemini 3.0 Pro: The authors developed SIMPO (Simplified Intelligent Modelling Platform Online), a free, open-source, cloud-based SaaS platform. Solutions include:

  1. Code-free GUI: Features a drag-and-drop interface and visual editing of stoichiometric matrices (similar to ASM tables) to simplify model construction.
  2. Intelligent Algorithms: Uses adaptive solvers (Forward/Improved Euler) that optimize step sizes in real-time for accuracy and efficiency.
  3. Automated Error-Proofing: Includes automatic balance checking for stoichiometric matrices and immediate visualization of results to detect anomalies (e.g., negative concentrations).
  4. Integrated Advanced Tools: Provides built-in algorithms for sensitivity analysis (OAT), uncertainty assessment (Monte Carlo/LHS), and parameter estimation (Genetic Algorithms).
  5. Open Science Features: Supports cloud storage and sharing of models via links, facilitating peer review and collaboration.

Q: What experiments were done for the paper?

Claude-4.5-Opus-Thinking: The paper conducted several validation experiments and case studies:

1. Accuracy Validation Against AQUASIM‚Äã

  • Model: Growth model of biological nutrient removal organisms (10 components, 21 parameters, 13 processes)
  • Reactors: Batch reactor (150 hours) and Chemostat reactor (25,000 hours)
  • Results: WNSE = 1.0 for batch and 0.99994 for chemostat
  • Conclusion: SIMPO achieves identical accuracy to professional software AQUASIM 2.1

2. Error Detection Case Study - Extended ASM3 for N‚ÇÇO‚Äã

  • Model: Extended ASM3 model (19 components, 63 parameters, 30 processes) from Blomberg et al. (2018)
  • Errors detected:

    • Wrong yield coefficient in process p10b for S_O
    • Missing correct value for process p1 for S_NH
  • Method: Balance checking feature identified ThOD and N imbalances

3. Error Detection Case Study - Autotrophic Denitrification Model‚Äã

  • Model: AD model with sulfide as electron donor (6 components, 9 parameters, 4 processes) from Xu et al. (2016)
  • Issues identified:
    • Negative S‚Å∞ concentration (resolved by adding switching function)

4. Sensitivity Analysis Demonstration‚Äã

  • Applied OAT analysis to AD model
  • Generated parameter ranking and correlation heat map
  • Results consistent with original study

5. Uncertainty Assessment Demonstration‚Äã

  • 1,000,000 LHS samples for AD model
  • Evaluated parameter distributions, equifinality, and model output ranges

Q: What are the points that can be explored further?

Claude-4.5-Opus-Thinking:

Technical Improvements‚Äã

  1. GPU Parallel Computing: Currently SIMPO only supports CPU parallel computing; GPU acceleration would significantly improve computational efficiency for large-scale simulations.

  2. Extended Reactor Types: The platform currently supports only continuous stirred tank reactors (CSTR) and point settling tanks. Future work should include:

  • Plug flow reactors (PFR)
  • Sequencing batch reactors (SBR)
  • Membrane bioreactors (MBR)
  • Biofilm reactors
  1. Expanded Model Libraries: More built-in biochemical models beyond ASM series, such as:
  • Anaerobic digestion models (ADM1)
  • Partial nitritation-anammox models
  • Microalgae-bacteria consortium models

AI Integration‚Äã

  1. DeepSeek API Integration: Enable linking classic mechanism-driven modelling with AI-assisted data-driven modelling.

  2. Intelligent Model Recommendation: AI-powered suggestions for model selection and parameter initialization based on input data characteristics.

  3. Automated Model Calibration: Machine learning-assisted parameter estimation combining mechanistic understanding with data patterns.

Platform Enhancement‚Äã

  1. Real-time Data Integration: Connect with SCADA systems for real-time operational optimization.

  2. Multi-objective Optimization: Extend parameter estimation to handle multiple competing objectives (e.g., energy, cost, effluent quality).

  3. Physical-chemical Process Models: Expand beyond biological processes to include chemical precipitation, adsorption, and membrane filtration.

Research Applications‚Äã

  1. Hybrid Modelling: Combine mechanistic ASM-type models with data-driven neural network approaches.

  2. Digital Twin Development: Use SIMPO as foundation for wastewater treatment plant digital twins.

  3. Climate Impact Assessment: Integrate greenhouse gas emission models for sustainability analysis.

Gemini 3.0 Pro: The authors identified specific limitations and future directions:

  1. GPU Acceleration: Currently, parallel computing is CPU-based. Future versions plan to support GPU parallel computing to speed up sensitivity and uncertainty calculations.
  2. Reactor Types: The platform is currently limited to Continuous Stirred Tank Reactors (CSTR) and point settling tanks. More reactor types need to be added.
  3. Library Expansion: The built-in libraries for models and process layouts are limited and require expansion.
  4. AI Integration: The paper suggests future API integration with AI tools like DeepSeek to link mechanism-driven modeling with AI-assisted data-driven modeling.
